## 7. If You Get Denied â€” What To Do

Denials are common and often wrong. **Do not accept a denial without reviewing it with your specialist.**

### Common Denial Reasons and Responses

| Denial Reason | What It Means | What To Do |
|--------------|--------------|-----------|
| "Income too high" | Your reported income exceeds the threshold | Verify they used current (not prior year) income. Request recalculation. |
| "Missing documentation" | They didn't receive or can't find your paperwork | Re-submit with delivery confirmation. Keep copies of everything. |
| "Not eligible in your state" | Program rules vary by state | Check if a similar state-level program exists. Ask your specialist. |
| "Separation was voluntary" | Employer reported you quit | File an appeal with evidence of constructive dismissal or lay-off. Your specialist can help draft the appeal letter. |
| "Assets exceed limit" | Savings or property disqualifies you | Some programs exempt primary residence and vehicles. Verify asset calculation. |

### Appeal Timeline

Most programs give you **30-90 days** to appeal a denial. Your specialist will:
1. Review the denial letter with you
2. Identify the specific reason and counter-evidence
3. Help you draft or file the appeal
4. Prepare you for any phone hearing (if required)
