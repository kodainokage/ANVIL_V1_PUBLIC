## 5. Negotiation Strategy

*If dispute letters don't fully resolve the bills, use this playbook when calling billing departments.*

### Before You Call

- [ ] Have your itemized bill in front of you
- [ ] Have this audit report open for reference
- [ ] Have a pen and paper ready to document the call
- [ ] Know the fair market price for each service (listed in Section 3)
- [ ] Call during off-peak hours (Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-2pm tends to work best)

### The Call Script

**Step 1: Get the right person**
> "Hi, I need to speak with someone who has authority to adjust billing charges or set up a financial hardship arrangement. Can you transfer me to a billing supervisor or patient financial services?"

**Step 2: Document everything**
> "Before we begin, can I get your name, title, and a direct callback number?" *(Write it down.)*

**Step 3: Present the errors**
> "I've had my bill professionally audited and we found `[X]` errors totaling `$[AMOUNT]`. I'm going to walk through each one."
> *(Go through each finding from Section 3, calmly and factually.)*

**Step 4: Request a specific resolution**
> "Based on the errors and fair market rates for these services, the correct amount should be `$[AMOUNT]`. I'd like to resolve this today."

**Step 5: If they push back**

| They say... | You say... |
|------------|-----------|
| "That's our standard charge" | "I understand, but Medicare reimburses `$[X]` for this same service. I'd like to discuss a fair adjustment." |
| "I can't adjust that" | "Who in your organization has the authority to review billing disputes? I'd like to speak with them or submit a formal written dispute." |
| "We'll review and get back to you" | "I appreciate that. Can I get a reference number and a specific date by which I'll hear back? I'll follow up if I don't hear by then." |
| "We can offer X% off" | "Thank you for the offer. Based on my audit, the fair amount is `$[X]`. Can you meet me closer to that number?" |
| "This has been sent to collections" | "Please recall it from collections while we resolve this dispute. I've also sent a debt validation letter to the collections agency." |

**Step 6: Get it in writing**
> "Can you send me written confirmation of `[the adjustment / the agreement / the corrected bill]` to my email at `[EMAIL]`?"

### Negotiation Leverage Points

- **Cash-pay discount:** Most hospitals offer 30-60% discounts for uninsured or self-pay patients. Ask: "What is your uninsured/self-pay rate for this service?"
- **Prompt-pay discount:** Offer to pay the corrected amount immediately for an additional 10-20% discount.
- **Medicare comparison:** Medicare rates are public. If you're being charged 5-10x the Medicare rate, that is powerful leverage.
- **Charity care threshold:** If your income is below 200-400% of the Federal Poverty Level, you may qualify for free or reduced-cost care retroactively.
- **Media and reviews:** You generally should not threaten this directly, but billing departments know that unresolved disputes can become public complaints.

| **Application deadline** | `[Typically 240 days from first billing statement]` |
| **What to submit** | Pay stubs (or unemployment verification), tax return, bank statements, hardship letter |

### Payment Plan Negotiation

If you don't qualify for charity care:
- Request a **0% interest payment plan** (most hospitals offer this)
- Propose a monthly amount you can afford: `$[SUGGESTED AMOUNT]`
- **Never agree to a plan you can't maintain** — a defaulted payment plan can go straight to collections
- Get the payment plan terms **in writing** before making the first payment

### Medical Credit Cards (Caution)

- CareCredit and similar medical credit cards offer 0% promotional periods
- **WARNING:** If the balance is not paid in full by the promo deadline, you are charged interest retroactively on the full original amount (often 26-29% APR)
- Generally **not recommended** unless you are 100% certain you can pay it off within the promotional period

| #3 | `[PROVIDER]` | `[FACILITY]` | `[SITUATION]` | `[Yes / No / Likely]` | `[EXPLANATION]` |

### NSA-Protected Scenarios

The No Surprises Act applies when:
- [x] Emergency services at any facility (in-network or out-of-network)
- [x] Non-emergency services at an in-network facility from an out-of-network provider you did not choose
- [x] Air ambulance services from out-of-network providers

If the NSA applies, **you should only owe your in-network cost-sharing amount** (copay, coinsurance, deductible). The provider cannot balance-bill you for the rest.

### How to File an NSA Complaint

If a provider is balance-billing you in violation of the NSA:

1. **CMS Complaint:** Call 1-800-985-3059 or visit cms.gov/nosurprises
2. **State Insurance Department:** `[STATE INSURANCE DEPT URL and PHONE]`
3. **Provider Notice:** Send a written notice citing 42 U.S.C. 300gg-111 (we can draft this for you)
