**Document Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

This document maps ANVIL's three career pathways to Standard Occupational Classification (SOC) codes as required for Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL) applications. Correct SOC code alignment is critical: an incorrect or unsupported SOC code is a common cause of ETPL application rejection. Each pathway is mapped to primary and secondary SOC codes, with supporting rationale, O*NET descriptions, wage data, and growth projections.

All wage data is sourced from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS) program. Growth projections are from the BLS Employment Projections program. O*NET descriptions are from the National Center for O*NET Development (onetonline.org).

## 1. Pathway 1: Government Benefits Navigation

### 1.1 Primary SOC Code

**SOC 21-1093: Social and Human Service Assistants**

| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| **SOC Code** | 21-1093 |
| **O*NET Code** | 21-1093.00 |
| **Title** | Social and Human Service Assistants |
| **Description** | Assist in providing client services in a wide variety of fields, such as psychology, rehabilitation, or social work, including support for families. May assist clients in identifying and obtaining available benefits and social and community services. May assist social workers with developing, organizing, and conducting programs to prevent and resolve problems relevant to substance abuse, human relationships, rehabilitation, or dependent care. |
| **Key Tasks Aligned to ANVIL** | Assess clients' needs, determine eligibility for services, and help them obtain services; assist clients in completing applications; provide information and referrals to other social service agencies and community resources; keep records and prepare reports; interview clients to gather information about their situations |
| **Education Typically Required** | High school diploma or equivalent (entry level); some positions prefer or require postsecondary certificate or associate degree |
| **Median Annual Wage (2024)** | $39,790 |
| **Mean Annual Wage (2024)** | $42,550 |
| **10th Percentile** | $29,200 |
| **90th Percentile** | $58,600 |
| **National Employment (2022)** | 397,100 |
| **Projected Growth (2022-2032)** | 12% (much faster than average) |
| **Projected New Jobs (2022-2032)** | 46,900 |

**Alignment Rationale:** ANVIL Benefits Navigation graduates perform the core functions described in this SOC code: assessing client needs, determining program eligibility, assisting with applications, providing referrals, and maintaining client records. The AI-augmented approach does not change the occupational classification; it enhances the efficiency of tasks within the existing occupational definition.

### 1.2 Secondary SOC Code

**SOC 43-4061: Eligibility Interviewers, Government Programs**

| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| **SOC Code** | 43-4061 |
| **O*NET Code** | 43-4061.00 |
| **Title** | Eligibility Interviewers, Government Programs |
| **Description** | Determine eligibility of persons applying to receive assistance from government programs and agency resources, such as welfare, unemployment benefits, social security, and public housing. |
| **Key Tasks Aligned to ANVIL** | Interview applicants to determine eligibility for assistance; explain programs and procedures; compute and authorize amounts of assistance; prepare reports and case records; initiate procedures to grant, modify, deny, or terminate assistance |
| **Education Typically Required** | High school diploma or equivalent; some positions require postsecondary education |
| **Median Annual Wage (2024)** | $49,140 |
| **Mean Annual Wage (2024)** | $50,510 |
| **10th Percentile** | $34,400 |
| **90th Percentile** | $70,000 |
| **National Employment (2022)** | 157,400 |
| **Projected Growth (2022-2032)** | -2% (little or no change) |
| **Projected New Jobs (2022-2032)** | Decline of approximately 2,800 |

**Alignment Rationale:** This SOC code describes the government-side function that ANVIL graduates assist with from the client side. While graduates do not work as government employees determining eligibility, they perform closely analogous functions: interviewing clients, assessing eligibility against program criteria, computing potential benefits, and preparing application documentation. This secondary code supports the occupational relevance of the training, particularly for graduates who may be hired by government agencies or their contractors.

**Note on Projected Decline:** The BLS projection of -2% growth for this code reflects consolidation of government intake functions, not reduced demand for the underlying service. The demand for benefits navigation assistance is increasing; it is the delivery model (more community-based, less government-office-based) that is shifting. This shift supports the ANVIL model of community-based, technology-augmented navigation.

## 2. Pathway 2: Medical Bill Auditing

**SOC 29-2072: Medical Records Specialists**

| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| **SOC Code** | 29-2072 |
| **O*NET Code** | 29-2072.00 |
| **Title** | Medical Records Specialists |
| **Description** | Compile, process, and maintain medical records of hospital and clinic patients in a manner consistent with medical, administrative, ethical, legal, and regulatory requirements of the healthcare system. Classify medical and healthcare concepts, including diagnosis, procedures, medical services, and equipment, into the healthcare industry's numerical coding system. Includes medical coders. |
| **Key Tasks Aligned to ANVIL** | Review medical records for accuracy and completeness; assign and verify codes for diagnosis and procedure classifications; analyze records to determine charges; compile medical care information; resolve billing discrepancies and disputes |
| **Education Typically Required** | Postsecondary nondegree award (certificates in medical coding/billing are common); some positions accept equivalent experience or alternative credentials |
| **Median Annual Wage (2024)** | $48,780 |
| **Mean Annual Wage (2024)** | $51,290 |
| **10th Percentile** | $32,100 |
| **90th Percentile** | $75,100 |
| **National Employment (2022)** | 216,200 |
| **Projected Growth (2022-2032)** | 7% (faster than average) |
| **Projected New Jobs (2022-2032)** | 14,800 |

**Alignment Rationale:** ANVIL Medical Bill Auditing graduates analyze medical bills, verify coding accuracy, identify billing discrepancies, and resolve disputes. While traditional medical records specialists work within healthcare organizations, the ANVIL graduate performs the same analytical functions from the patient advocacy side. The core competencies -- understanding CPT/HCPCS codes, reviewing itemized bills, identifying errors, and working with billing departments -- are directly aligned.

### 2.2 Secondary SOC Code

| **Title** | Claims Adjusters, Examiners, and Investigators |
| **Description** | Review settled claims to determine that payments and settlements are made in accordance with company practices and procedures. Examine claims investigated by insurance adjusters, examiners, and investigators. May also settle insurance claims. |
| **Key Tasks Aligned to ANVIL** | Examine claims forms and other records to determine insurance coverage; investigate questionable claims; negotiate claim settlements; calculate amount of claim; correspond with claimants regarding claim settlement |
| **Education Typically Required** | High school diploma or equivalent for many positions; bachelor's degree preferred for some |
| **Median Annual Wage (2024)** | $75,020 |
| **Mean Annual Wage (2024)** | $77,120 |
| **10th Percentile** | $44,200 |
| **90th Percentile** | $109,300 |
| **National Employment (2022)** | 326,200 |
| **Projected Growth (2022-2032)** | -3% (decline) |
| **Projected New Jobs (2022-2032)** | Decline of approximately 10,900 |

**Alignment Rationale:** ANVIL graduates who focus on insurance claims disputes, balance billing under the No Surprises Act, and insurance appeal preparation perform functions analogous to claims examination. They review charges against contractual obligations, identify discrepancies, calculate appropriate amounts, and negotiate resolutions. This code is particularly relevant for graduates who transition into employment with insurance companies, third-party administrators, or patient advocacy firms.

**Note on Projected Decline:** The -3% projection reflects automation of routine claims processing. The ANVIL model addresses this directly: graduates are trained to handle the complex, non-routine claims disputes that automated systems cannot resolve, positioning them in the segment of the occupation that is growing, not declining.

## 3. Pathway 3: Small Business Compliance Assistance

### 3.1 Primary SOC Code

**SOC 13-1041: Compliance Officers**

| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| **SOC Code** | 13-1041 |
| **O*NET Code** | 13-1041.00 |
| **Title** | Compliance Officers |
| **Description** | Examine, evaluate, and investigate eligibility for or conformity with laws and regulations governing contract compliance of licenses and permits, and other compliance activities. Excludes Financial Examiners (13-2061) and Tax Examiners and Collectors, and Revenue Agents (13-2081). |
| **Key Tasks Aligned to ANVIL** | Evaluate compliance with applicable laws and regulations; identify areas of non-compliance; prepare reports detailing findings; advise businesses on regulatory requirements; maintain current knowledge of industry regulations; conduct audits or reviews |
| **Education Typically Required** | Bachelor's degree (for many positions); however, entry-level compliance support roles and self-employed compliance consultants often require demonstrated knowledge and experience rather than a specific degree |
| **Median Annual Wage (2024)** | $75,670 |
| **Mean Annual Wage (2024)** | $80,570 |
| **10th Percentile** | $44,500 |
| **90th Percentile** | $124,400 |
| **National Employment (2022)** | 354,800 |
| **Projected Growth (2022-2032)** | 4% (as fast as average) |
| **Projected New Jobs (2022-2032)** | 14,600 |

**Alignment Rationale:** ANVIL Small Business Compliance graduates perform the core functions of compliance officers in a consulting capacity: they evaluate businesses for compliance with applicable laws and regulations (permits, licenses, registrations, tax obligations), identify areas of non-compliance, prepare detailed compliance reports, and advise businesses on regulatory requirements. The difference is the delivery model (external consultant serving small businesses) rather than the occupational function itself.

**Note on Wage Data:** The median wage of $75,670 reflects the full range of compliance officer positions, including those at large corporations requiring advanced degrees and specialized certifications. Entry-level and self-employed compliance consultants serving small businesses operate at the lower end of the wage distribution ($44,500--$60,000 range), which is consistent with ANVIL's projected earnings for this pathway.

## 4. Summary: Recommended SOC Codes for ETPL Application

| Pathway | Primary SOC | Secondary SOC | Recommended for ETPL Application |
|---------|-------------|---------------|----------------------------------|
| Benefits Navigation | 21-1093 (Social and Human Service Assistants) | 43-4061 (Eligibility Interviewers) | **21-1093** |
| Medical Bill Auditing | 29-2072 (Medical Records Specialists) | 13-1031 (Claims Adjusters) | **29-2072** |
| Small Business Compliance | 13-1041 (Compliance Officers) | 13-1199 (Business Operations Specialists) | **13-1041** |

**Application Strategy:** Most state ETPL applications require one or two SOC codes per program or program track. If the application allows listing multiple tracks (pathways) under a single program, list all three primary codes. If the application requires a single SOC code for the entire program, use **21-1093 (Social and Human Service Assistants)** as it has the broadest applicability, the strongest growth projection (12%), and the lowest entry barrier (high school diploma or equivalent), which best matches the ANVIL program's target population.

If the state requires separate ETPL applications for each career pathway, submit three applications using the primary SOC code for each pathway.
