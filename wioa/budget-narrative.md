## 2. Line-Item Budget with Narrative Justification

### Category A: Personnel

| Position | FTE | Annual Salary | Total Cost |
|----------|-----|---------------|-----------|
| Lead Instructor | [0.5--1.0] | $[50,000--70,000] | $[25,000--70,000] |
| Program Administrator | [0.25--0.5] | $[40,000--50,000] | $[10,000--25,000] |
| Community Manager / Follow-Up Coordinator | [0.25--0.5] | $[35,000--45,000] | $[8,750--22,500] |
| Payroll Taxes and Benefits (estimated at 20%) | -- | -- | $[8,750--23,500] |
| **Personnel Subtotal** | | | **$[52,500--141,000]** |

**Narrative:**

**Lead Instructor ($[25,000--70,000]):** The lead instructor is responsible for delivering all cohort sessions, preparing and updating curriculum materials, administering and scoring certification assessments, and providing real-time guidance during practice exercises. At 0.5 FTE, the instructor delivers approximately 2 cohorts per month (8 cohorts annually, approximately 200 participants). At 1.0 FTE, the instructor delivers 4 cohorts per month (up to 16 cohorts annually). The salary range reflects local labor market rates for experienced trainers and educators with subject matter expertise in at least one of the three career pathways.

**Program Administrator ($[10,000--25,000]):** The program administrator manages enrollment processing, participant file maintenance, coordination with referring American Job Centers and Local Workforce Development Boards, data entry into state workforce systems, scheduling, and host site logistics. At 0.25 FTE, this position supports approximately 8 cohorts annually. The role may be combined with the community manager role at smaller scale.

**Community Manager / Follow-Up Coordinator ($[8,750--22,500]):** This position is responsible for managing the post-program follow-up schedule, conducting 30/60/90/180-day check-in contacts with graduates, collecting employment and earnings data for WIOA reporting, maintaining the cohort communication channels, and coordinating peer mentorship. The follow-up function is critical to meeting WIOA reporting requirements and is often the most resource-intensive component of outcomes tracking.

**Payroll Taxes and Benefits:** Estimated at 20% of total salary costs to cover the employer share of FICA (7.65%), federal and state unemployment insurance, workers compensation insurance, and any applicable benefits.

| Backup and data security | $0--10 | $0--120 |
| **Technology Subtotal** | **$12--111** | **$12--1,200** |

**Narrative:**

ANVIL's technology infrastructure leverages free-tier services for the majority of its operational needs. This is a deliberate design decision, not a compromise: the program teaches participants to use free AI tools, so the program itself operates on those same tools.

- **Web hosting** runs on Render.com's free tier for the program website and quiz. A paid tier ($7--20/month) may be used for higher availability if traffic warrants.
- **Video conferencing** uses Google Meet (free with a Google account for meetings up to 60 minutes, or Google Workspace at $6--14/user/month for extended sessions). If cohorts are delivered in-person at host sites, video conferencing costs are $0.
- **AI tool subscriptions** for instructor demonstrations may require paid tiers ($20--40/month) to avoid rate limits during live sessions. Participant AI tool usage is exclusively on free tiers and incurs no program cost.
- **All participant-facing tools are free.** Participants do not pay for any software, platform, or subscription.

### Category D: Facilities and Host Site Costs

**Narrative:**

ANVIL's host site strategy prioritizes no-cost partnerships with public institutions:

- **Public libraries** frequently provide meeting room space at no charge for community education programs. Many libraries have existing projector and screen equipment.
- **Community colleges** offer classroom space for continuing education and workforce development partnerships, typically at no cost or nominal administrative fees.
- **American Job Centers** have dedicated space for training programs and are natural referral partners.
- **Nonprofit organizations** serving the target population often have meeting space available for complementary programs.

In locations where no free host site is available, rental of community center or coworking meeting rooms typically costs $50--200 per session.

Virtual cohort delivery eliminates all facility costs.

**Narrative:**

1. **Referrals from American Job Centers and WIOA case managers** -- the primary enrollment channel for WIOA-funded participants

The $0 marketing budget reflects the program's design: WIOA-funded participants are referred through the existing workforce development infrastructure, not recruited through commercial advertising.

## 5. Sustainability Plan

### 5.1 Revenue Diversification

ANVIL's long-term sustainability does not depend solely on WIOA funding. The program pursues multiple revenue streams:

| Revenue Source | Timeline | Description |
|---------------|----------|-------------|
| WIOA ITA reimbursements | Ongoing | Primary funding source for WIOA-eligible participants |
| WIOA direct contracts with LWDBs | Year 1+ | Group training contracts for cohort delivery |
| Community college partnerships | Year 1+ | Continuing education hosting arrangements with cost-sharing |
| Library system partnerships | Year 1+ | Grant-funded workshop delivery through library workforce programs |
| Nonprofit and foundation grants | Year 1+ | Grants from workforce development, digital inclusion, and community service foundations |
| Fee-for-service cohorts | Year 2+ | Paid cohorts ($49--149/participant) for individuals not eligible for WIOA funding |
| Employer-sponsored training | Year 2+ | Companies sponsor employee training as part of workforce transition programs |
| Marketplace commission | Year 2+ | Percentage-based commission on client engagements facilitated through the ANVIL specialist marketplace |

### 5.2 Financial Sustainability Milestones

| Milestone | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Break-even (revenue covers operating costs) | Month 6--12 |
| WIOA funding covers 100% of WIOA-participant costs | Month 3 (upon ETPL approval) |
| Non-WIOA revenue exceeds 25% of total revenue | Month 12--18 |
| Program self-sustaining without WIOA funding | Month 24--36 |

### 5.3 Cost Control Measures

- **Technology costs are locked near zero** through the use of free-tier commercial services. Even if these services change their pricing, alternatives are available at minimal cost.
- **Facilities costs are minimized** through partnerships with public institutions that provide space at no charge.
- **Marketing costs are minimized** through organic referral channels and integration with existing workforce development infrastructure.
- **Curriculum development costs decrease over time** as the core materials mature and require only annual updates rather than creation from scratch.
- **Per-participant costs decrease with scale** as fixed costs (personnel, technology, insurance) are distributed across more participants.

## 6. Budget Modifications

Any modifications to the approved budget that exceed 10% reallocation between categories will be submitted to the applicable LWDB or state workforce agency for prior approval, in accordance with the terms of the training provider agreement and applicable federal requirements at 2 CFR 200.308.

Minor reallocations within the 10% threshold will be documented internally and reported in the next quarterly financial report.

## 7. Audit Readiness

ANVIL maintains financial records sufficient to support an audit at any time, including:

- General ledger with all income and expenses categorized by budget line item
- Bank statements and reconciliations
- Receipts and invoices for all expenditures over $25
- Payroll records and tax filings
- Time and effort documentation for personnel charging time to the WIOA program
- Participant enrollment and completion records cross-referenced with financial transactions
- Cost allocation methodology documentation (if personnel or costs are shared across WIOA and non-WIOA activities)

Records are retained for a minimum of [3 / 5 / 7] years after the end of the applicable grant or contract period, or longer if required by state policy or pending audit.
