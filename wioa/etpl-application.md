## Section 2: Program Information

### 2.1 Program Name

**AI-Augmented Specialist Training Sprint**

### 2.2 Program Description

The ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Training Sprint is a 7-day, 19-hour intensive training program that prepares dislocated workers, long-term unemployed individuals, and underemployed adults to deliver professional services in high-demand community service niches using artificial intelligence tools. The program trains participants in one of three career pathways: Government Benefits Navigation, Medical Bill Auditing, or Small Business Compliance Assistance.

Participants learn to use commercially available AI platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity) as research accelerators while developing the critical human skills that AI cannot replicate: client intake and relationship management, output verification and quality assurance, ethical judgment and scope-of-practice compliance, and professional service delivery.

The program is delivered in a blended format combining live group instruction (via video conferencing or in-person at host sites), structured hands-on practice with real-world case simulations, and independent study with provided materials. Each participant completes a practical certification assessment on Day 7 demonstrating competency across intake, analysis, verification, ethics, quality assurance, and client communication.

### 2.3 Program Objectives

Upon completion, participants will be able to:

1. Conduct a structured client intake interview appropriate to their selected career pathway.
2. Use AI tools to research, analyze, and synthesize information relevant to client cases.
3. Verify all AI-generated outputs against authoritative primary sources.
4. Produce professional client-ready deliverables (eligibility reports, bill audit findings, compliance checklists).
5. Communicate findings to clients in plain language with appropriate disclaimers.
6. Identify ethical boundaries and scope-of-practice limitations for their career pathway.
7. Price services appropriately and manage a client engagement from intake through delivery and follow-up.
8. Identify and pursue client acquisition channels relevant to their career pathway and local market.

### 2.5 Career Pathway Details

**Pathway 1: Government Benefits Navigation**
- SOC Codes: 21-1093 (Social and Human Service Assistants), 43-4061 (Eligibility Interviewers, Government Programs)
- Services: Eligibility screening for 20+ federal and state programs, application assistance, follow-up and recertification support
- Target clients: Individuals experiencing job loss, income reduction, or life transitions who are unaware of or unable to navigate available government assistance programs
- Expected starting compensation: $50--85 per case engagement; $65--85/hour for complex cases

**Pathway 2: Medical Bill Auditing**
- SOC Codes: 29-2072 (Medical Records Specialists), 13-1031 (Claims Adjusters, Examiners, and Investigators)
- Services: Itemized bill error analysis, fair market rate comparison, dispute letter preparation, provider negotiation, charity care application assistance
- Target clients: Individuals with medical debt seeking error identification and bill reduction
- Expected starting compensation: 25--33% of savings achieved (minimum $75 per engagement); $75--150 flat fee per bill audit

**Pathway 3: Small Business Compliance Assistance**
- SOC Codes: 13-1041 (Compliance Officers), 13-1199 (Business Operations Specialists, All Other)
- Services: Permit and license identification, tax registration analysis, zoning compliance verification, insurance requirements assessment, annual compliance calendar creation
- Target clients: New small business owners and existing businesses seeking compliance verification
- Expected starting compensation: $85--200 per compliance audit; $200--400 for audit plus application preparation

## Section 3: Admission Requirements

### 3.1 Eligibility Criteria

Participants must meet at least one of the following criteria:

- Dislocated worker as defined under WIOA Section 3(15)
- Adult (age 18+) who is unemployed or underemployed
- Recipient of public assistance (SNAP, TANF, SSI, Medicaid)
- Low-income individual as defined under WIOA Section 3(36)
- Individual with barriers to employment as identified by the referring American Job Center

### 3.2 Prerequisites

- Access to a laptop or desktop computer with reliable internet
- Access to a smartphone
- Ability to create free accounts on AI platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity)
- Functional literacy in English (reading and writing at approximately 8th-grade level)
- Availability to participate in scheduled sessions for 7 consecutive days

### 3.3 Equipment and Materials

No specialized equipment or software purchases are required. All tools used in the program are available at no cost on free-tier plans from commercial AI providers. Participants who do not own a laptop may access one through partner library systems or community organizations.

## Section 6: Expected Outcomes

### 6.1 WIOA Primary Indicators of Performance

| Performance Measure | Target |
|---------------------|--------|
| Program Completion Rate | 75% or higher |
| Credential Attainment Rate (ANVIL Specialist Certification) | 70% of completers |
| Employment Rate, 2nd Quarter After Exit | 55% or higher |
| Employment Rate, 4th Quarter After Exit | 50% or higher |
| Median Earnings, 2nd Quarter After Exit | $5,500 or higher |
| Measurable Skill Gains | 80% of participants |

### 6.2 Basis for Outcome Projections

**Completion Rate (75%):** The program's 7-day duration and zero-cost structure minimize the attrition factors common in longer programs (childcare burden, transportation costs, competing employment). Cohort sizes of 10--25 create accountability and community support. The estimate accounts for approximately 25% attrition due to personal circumstances, change of interest, or inability to meet the daily time commitment.

**Employment Rate (55% at Q2, 50% at Q4):** These projections reflect the program's focus on self-employment and freelance service delivery, which is not captured in all standard employment measurement methodologies. Graduates who are actively serving clients on a freelance basis are counted as employed. The Q4 figure accounts for the reality that some graduates will transition to traditional employment, while others may discontinue freelance work. These targets are conservative relative to national WIOA averages for Adult program participants (approximately 75% employment rate in Q2), as the program serves a population with multiple barriers and the career pathways involve nontraditional employment.

**Median Earnings ($5,500 at Q2):** Based on conservative projections of graduates serving 3--5 clients per week at $50--150 per engagement during the ramp-up period of months 1--3. This figure does not include graduates who obtain traditional full-time employment in related fields, which would increase the median.

**Credential Attainment (70%):** The ANVIL Specialist Certification is a performance-based assessment requiring demonstration of competency across 10 practical domains. The 70% target reflects the assessment's rigor; participants who do not pass on the first attempt may retake the assessment within 30 days.

**Measurable Skill Gains (80%):** Measured through pre- and post-program skills assessments administered on Day 1 (baseline) and Day 7 (completion). Assessment domains include AI tool proficiency, niche-specific knowledge, client communication, ethical reasoning, and quality assurance practices.
