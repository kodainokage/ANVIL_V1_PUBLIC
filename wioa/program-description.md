## 1. Mission Statement

ANVIL exists to transition workers displaced by artificial intelligence into AI-augmented service specialists who serve their communities in high-demand, underserved niches. The program provides free, intensive training that equips participants to deliver professional-grade services in government benefits navigation, medical bill auditing, and small business compliance assistance -- fields where AI tools accelerate research but cannot replace the human judgment, empathy, and verification that clients require.

---

## 2. Program Overview

The ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Training Sprint is a 7-day, 19-hour structured training program that prepares adults for immediate entry into one of three career pathways. The program is delivered at no cost to participants and is designed for funding through the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) Title I Adult and Dislocated Worker programs.

The program addresses a structural gap in the current workforce development landscape: workers displaced by AI adoption need rapid reskilling into roles where AI serves as a tool rather than a replacement. ANVIL identifies service niches characterized by strong community demand, suitability for AI-assisted workflows, and low barriers to entry, then trains participants to operate effectively within those niches.

**Key Program Characteristics:**
- Duration: 7 consecutive days, 19 hours of structured instruction
- Cost to participant: $0
- Delivery: In-person at host sites, live virtual, or hybrid
- Cohort size: 10--25 participants
- Credential: ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Certification (performance-based)
- Employment model: Self-employment/freelance service delivery, with pathways to traditional employment

## 3. Target Population

### 3.1 Primary Populations Served

**Dislocated Workers (WIOA Section 3(15)):** Individuals who have been terminated or laid off from employment, particularly those whose displacement is attributable to automation, AI adoption, or organizational restructuring driven by technology. This includes workers from administrative, clerical, customer service, data entry, and other roles where AI tools have reduced employer demand for human labor.

**Long-Term Unemployed:** Individuals who have been unemployed for 27 weeks or longer and have exhausted or are not eligible for unemployment insurance benefits. This population often faces compounding barriers as the duration of unemployment increases.

**Underemployed Adults:** Individuals employed in positions substantially below their skill level or working fewer hours than desired, including those who have accepted survival employment after displacement from career-track positions.

**Low-Income Adults:** Individuals meeting the WIOA low-income definition under Section 3(36), including those receiving public assistance, those whose income does not exceed the higher of the poverty line or 70% of the lower living standard income level, and individuals who are homeless.

**Individuals with Barriers to Employment:** Including but not limited to individuals with disabilities, older workers (55+), formerly incarcerated individuals, single parents, individuals with limited English proficiency, and individuals lacking a high school diploma or equivalent (provided they meet functional literacy requirements for program participation).

### 3.2 Participant Profile

The typical ANVIL participant is an adult between the ages of 25 and 60 who has experienced involuntary job loss or significant income reduction within the past 24 months. Many participants report anxiety about the role of AI in their career trajectory and have attempted traditional job search methods (applications, job fairs, resume revision) without success. Participants typically possess functional literacy and computer skills sufficient for internet-based work, but lack exposure to AI tools and lack a clear pathway to leverage AI rather than compete with it.

The program does not require any specific educational credential, professional license, or technical background. Participants do not need prior experience with AI, medical billing, government programs, or business compliance.

## 4. Training Methodology

### 4.1 The AI-Augmented Specialist Model

ANVIL's training methodology is built on a foundational premise: in an increasing number of professional service fields, AI tools can perform 70--90% of the research and analysis work, but cannot replace the human functions of client relationship management, output verification, ethical judgment, and plain-language communication. By training participants to operate at the intersection of AI capability and human necessity, the program creates specialists who are more productive than traditional practitioners and more reliable than unsupervised AI.

The model consists of four core competencies taught in sequence:

1. **AI Tool Proficiency:** Participants learn to use commercially available AI platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity) effectively for research, analysis, and document generation. Instruction covers prompt engineering principles, multi-platform verification, and output quality assessment.

2. **Niche-Specific Domain Knowledge:** Through structured playbooks specific to each career pathway, participants acquire working knowledge of the regulations, processes, and client needs in their chosen niche. This knowledge is sufficient for competent service delivery and for identifying situations requiring referral to licensed professionals.

3. **Verification and Quality Assurance:** Participants learn systematic methods for verifying AI-generated outputs against authoritative primary sources. This includes specific verification protocols for dollar amounts, eligibility thresholds, regulatory citations, URLs, and program availability.

4. **Client Service Delivery:** Participants learn professional intake procedures, service agreements, communication scripts, ethical boundaries, data privacy practices, pricing strategies, and follow-up protocols.

### 4.2 Instructional Methods

- **Live group instruction:** Daily sessions delivered by a qualified instructor via video conference or in-person, covering concepts, demonstrations, and group discussion
- **Supervised practice:** Structured case simulations of increasing complexity, completed during sessions with instructor guidance
- **Peer review:** Participants exchange deliverables and provide structured feedback using provided checklists
- **Independent practice:** Homework assignments that reinforce session content and build the participant's personal prompt library and service documentation
- **Role-play exercises:** Simulated client interactions for intake, delivery, and ethical scenarios
- **Certification assessment:** A practical, performance-based assessment on Day 7

## 5. Curriculum Overview

### 5.1 Seven-Day Structure

**Day 1: AI Is Your New Co-Worker (2.5 hours)**
Participants gain a realistic understanding of AI capabilities and limitations. They set up free accounts on four AI platforms, complete their first AI-assisted research task, and explore the seven available career niches. The session establishes the program's central principle: AI is a research assistant, not an authority, and every output requires human verification.

**Day 2: Your Niche Deep Dive (2.5 hours)**
Participants officially select their career pathway from the three primary niches. They develop a detailed client avatar, learn the four-step service delivery model (intake, AI-assisted analysis, human review, client delivery), and complete a role-play client intake exercise with a practice partner or AI simulation.

**Day 3: The AI Workflow (3.0 hours)**
Participants learn prompt engineering fundamentals and build their personal prompt library -- a curated set of 8--12 niche-specific prompts optimized through iterative refinement. They complete three practice cases of escalating complexity (simple, medium, complex) through the full service delivery workflow.

**Day 4: Quality and Ethics (2.5 hours)**
The program's most critical session addresses legal and ethical boundaries, scope-of-practice limitations, quality assurance procedures, data privacy obligations, and professional liability. Participants work through five ethical scenario exercises and draft their service agreement using a provided template.

**Day 5: Finding Clients (2.5 hours)**
Participants identify the specific client acquisition channels relevant to their niche and local market. They write platform-specific outreach posts, learn the referral partnership model, develop a free-first-case strategy, and post at least one client-attracting message on a live platform before the session ends.

**Day 6: Delivering Value (3.0 hours)**
Participants complete a full timed practice case from intake to deliverable, participate in peer review, learn client communication scripts for expectation-setting, delivery, and follow-up, establish their pricing structure, and begin building their case portfolio.

**Day 7: Launch Day (3.0 hours)**
Participants take the ANVIL Certification Assessment (a 10-question practical evaluation). Those who pass set up their professional presence (Google Business profile, scheduling tool, payment method), receive their 30-day growth plan, and either serve their first real client or commit to a concrete plan to secure one within 72 hours.

### 5.2 Materials Provided

All curriculum materials are provided at no cost, including:
- Niche-specific playbooks (comprehensive practitioner guides for each career pathway)
- Practice case files (3 per niche at varying complexity levels)
- Prompt library templates
- Service agreement template
- Quality assurance checklists
- Outreach message templates
- Client intake forms
- Deliverable templates
- 30-day growth plan template
- Certification assessment

## 6. Career Pathways

### 6.1 Pathway 1: Government Benefits Navigation

**Description:** Benefits navigators help individuals and families identify, apply for, and maintain eligibility for government assistance programs. Using AI tools, navigators screen clients against 20+ federal and state programs, prepare applications, and provide ongoing recertification support.

**Services Provided:**
- Comprehensive eligibility screening across federal, state, and local programs (SNAP, Medicaid, TANF, LIHEAP, SSI/SSDI, WIOA, WIC, and others)
- Application preparation and submission assistance
- Follow-up on pending applications
- Recertification and renewal support
- Appeals assistance for denied applications
- Referral to legal aid and licensed professionals when appropriate

**Employment Opportunities:**
- Self-employed community benefits navigator
- Benefits specialist at nonprofit organizations
- Intake coordinator at community action agencies
- Navigator at American Job Centers
- Patient financial counselor at healthcare facilities

**Compensation Range:** $50--125 per client engagement (self-employed); $35,000--50,000 annually (employed positions)

**Occupational Alignment:** SOC 21-1093 (Social and Human Service Assistants), SOC 43-4061 (Eligibility Interviewers, Government Programs)

### 6.2 Pathway 2: Medical Bill Auditing

**Description:** Medical bill auditors review itemized medical bills for errors, overcharges, and billing code irregularities. They compare charges against fair market rates, identify applicable consumer protections, and prepare dispute documentation to reduce patient financial liability.

**Services Provided:**
- Itemized bill error analysis (upcoding, duplicates, unbundling, incorrect charges)
- Fair market rate comparison using Medicare fee schedules and FAIR Health data
- Legal protection analysis (No Surprises Act, state balance billing laws, FDCPA)
- Dispute letter preparation
- Provider negotiation
- Charity care and financial assistance application support
- Collection agency response and debt validation

**Employment Opportunities:**
- Self-employed medical billing advocate
- Patient advocate at hospitals and health systems
- Revenue integrity analyst at healthcare organizations
- Claims analyst at insurance companies
- Medical billing specialist at physician practices

**Compensation Range:** 25--33% of savings achieved per engagement (self-employed); $38,000--55,000 annually (employed positions)

**Occupational Alignment:** SOC 29-2072 (Medical Records Specialists), SOC 13-1031 (Claims Adjusters, Examiners, and Investigators)

### 6.3 Pathway 3: Small Business Compliance Assistance

**Description:** Compliance assistants audit small businesses to identify every required permit, license, registration, tax obligation, and insurance requirement at the federal, state, county, and city levels, then deliver a clear action plan the business owner can follow.

**Services Provided:**
- Comprehensive compliance audit across all government levels
- Permit and license identification with application instructions
- Tax registration analysis (income, sales, payroll, franchise)
- Zoning compliance verification
- Insurance requirements assessment
- Application preparation and submission
- Annual compliance calendar creation
- Ongoing compliance monitoring (retainer model)

**Employment Opportunities:**
- Self-employed compliance consultant
- Business compliance analyst at accounting firms
- Licensing specialist at municipal government
- Small business advisor at Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs)
- Compliance coordinator at business membership organizations

**Compensation Range:** $85--400 per engagement (self-employed); $40,000--60,000 annually (employed positions)

**Occupational Alignment:** SOC 13-1041 (Compliance Officers), SOC 13-1199 (Business Operations Specialists, All Other)

## 8. Quality Assurance Measures

### 8.1 Curriculum Quality

- Curriculum is reviewed and updated quarterly to reflect changes in AI tool capabilities, government program rules, medical billing regulations, and business compliance requirements.
- All niche-specific playbooks contain verification protocols requiring practitioners to confirm every fact, dollar amount, and URL against authoritative primary sources before client delivery.
- Practice cases are derived from realistic scenarios and are updated annually to reflect current-year thresholds, rates, and regulations.

### 8.2 Instructor Quality

- All instructors must complete the ANVIL Instructor Certification, which includes observation of at least two cohort deliveries and demonstrated proficiency in all three career pathways.
- Participant evaluations of instructor effectiveness are collected after each cohort and reviewed by program leadership.
- Instructors participate in annual continuing education to maintain currency with AI tool developments and niche-specific regulatory changes.

### 8.3 Assessment Integrity

- The ANVIL Certification Assessment is a standardized 10-question practical evaluation. Questions are drawn from a bank of 50+ scenarios and rotated across cohorts to prevent sharing.
- Assessments are scored against a detailed rubric by the instructor. Borderline cases (scores of 7/10) are reviewed by a second assessor.
- Participants who do not pass may retake the assessment once within 30 days after completing remedial activities identified by the assessor.

### 8.4 Continuous Improvement

- Post-cohort participant surveys capture satisfaction, perceived preparedness, and suggestions for improvement.
- Employment and earnings follow-up surveys are conducted at 30, 90, and 180 days post-completion.
- Program leadership reviews aggregate performance data quarterly and implements curriculum modifications as warranted by outcome trends.

## 9. Program Completion Requirements

To receive the ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Certification, a participant must:

1. **Attend a minimum of 5 of 7 scheduled sessions.** Absence from more than 2 sessions requires completion of documented make-up work covering missed content.

2. **Complete all assigned practice cases.** A minimum of 3 practice cases must be completed and submitted for review (Days 3 and 6).

3. **Draft a service agreement.** The service agreement must address scope of services, disclaimers, data privacy, and fee structure (Day 4).

4. **Post at least one client acquisition message on a live platform.** This demonstrates the participant's ability to translate training into action (Day 5).

5. **Pass the ANVIL Certification Assessment with a score of 8/10 or higher.** The assessment evaluates practical competency across intake, scope identification, AI workflow execution, verification, ethical reasoning, quality assurance, client communication, referral judgment, data privacy, and pricing (Day 7).

6. **Develop a 30-day growth plan.** The plan must identify specific client acquisition channels, partnership targets, and weekly activity commitments (Day 7).

Participants who meet requirements 1--4 and 6 but do not pass the certification assessment will receive a Certificate of Participation and may retake the assessment within 30 days.

## 10. Participant Support Services

ANVIL provides the following support services to facilitate participant success:

- **Pre-program orientation:** Overview of program expectations, technology requirements, and logistics
- **Cohort community access:** Private communication channel (Discord or equivalent) for peer support during and after the program
- **Post-program mentorship:** Graduates from prior cohorts serve as volunteer peer mentors for new graduates during their first 30 days of independent practice
- **Resource referrals:** For participants presenting needs outside the program's scope (housing instability, childcare, mental health, transportation), ANVIL provides referrals to local support services, including 211 hotline resources and American Job Center services
- **Follow-up check-ins:** Program staff conduct follow-up contacts at 30, 60, and 90 days post-completion to track outcomes and provide guidance

ANVIL does not directly provide supportive services such as transportation assistance, childcare, or needs-based payments. Participants requiring such services are referred to the applicable Local Workforce Development Board or American Job Center for WIOA supportive services.

## 11. Disclaimers

- ANVIL is a training program. It does not provide legal advice, medical advice, financial advice, or tax advice.
- Graduates of the program are not licensed professionals unless they independently hold applicable professional licenses.
- The career pathways described in this document involve service roles where licensing requirements vary by state and municipality. ANVIL instructs all participants on scope-of-practice limitations and the obligation to refer clients to licensed professionals when appropriate.
- Employment and earnings outcomes described in this document are targets and projections, not guarantees.
- The ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Certification is issued by ANVIL and is not a state-issued credential or a credential issued by a nationally recognized accrediting body, unless and until such recognition is obtained.
