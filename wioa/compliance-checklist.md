## 1. Equal Opportunity and Nondiscrimination

### 1.1 Policy Requirements

- [ ] Written nondiscrimination and equal opportunity policy adopted by the organization, covering all WIOA-protected categories: race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, sex stereotyping, transgender status, and gender identity), national origin, age, disability, political affiliation or belief, and for beneficiaries/applicants/participants, citizenship status and participation in a WIOA-funded program
- [ ] Policy references WIOA Section 188 and 29 CFR Part 38
- [ ] Policy is signed by the authorized organizational representative
- [ ] Policy has been reviewed and updated within the past 12 months

### 1.2 Notice and Communication

- [ ] Equal Opportunity notice posted in a conspicuous location at each training site (physical notice for in-person delivery)
- [ ] Equal Opportunity notice included in participant enrollment materials
- [ ] Equal Opportunity notice available on the program website
- [ ] Tagline included on all recruitment materials, brochures, and advertisements: "ANVIL is an equal opportunity employer/program. Auxiliary aids and services are available upon request to individuals with disabilities."
- [ ] Notice provided in languages other than English where a significant number of participants or potential participants are limited English proficient

### 1.3 Compliance Officer

- [ ] An individual has been designated as the program's Equal Opportunity Officer
- [ ] The EO Officer's name and contact information are included in all participant materials
- [ ] EO Officer: [NAME], [TITLE], [PHONE], [EMAIL]

## 2. ADA Accessibility Requirements

### 2.1 Physical Accessibility (In-Person Delivery)

- [ ] Training site is physically accessible to individuals with mobility impairments (ramps, elevators, accessible restrooms, adequate door widths)
- [ ] Training site complies with ADA Standards for Accessible Design
- [ ] Accessible parking is available at or near the training site
- [ ] Training room layout accommodates wheelchair users

### 2.2 Digital Accessibility (Virtual and Hybrid Delivery)

- [ ] Video conferencing platform supports screen readers and keyboard navigation
- [ ] Presentation materials use sufficient color contrast and font sizes (minimum 18pt for projected slides)
- [ ] Written materials are available in accessible formats (text-based PDF, not image-only PDF)
- [ ] Captioning is available or can be enabled during live virtual sessions

### 2.3 Reasonable Accommodations

- [ ] Written policy for requesting and providing reasonable accommodations for participants with disabilities
- [ ] Accommodation request process is communicated to all participants at enrollment
- [ ] Procedure for engaging in an interactive process with the participant to identify effective accommodations
- [ ] Accommodations provided within a reasonable timeframe (prior to or at the start of the program)
- [ ] Accommodation requests and dispositions are documented in the participant file

### 2.4 Programmatic Accessibility

- [ ] Certification assessment can be administered in alternative formats upon request (extended time, oral examination, assistive technology)
- [ ] Curriculum materials can be provided in large print, screen-reader-compatible electronic format, or other accessible formats
- [ ] Service animals are permitted at in-person training sites

## 3. Data Privacy and Participant Records

### 3.1 Privacy Policy

- [ ] Written privacy policy governing the collection, use, storage, and sharing of participant personally identifiable information (PII)
- [ ] Privacy policy complies with WIOA confidentiality requirements (20 CFR 677.175)
- [ ] Privacy policy addresses compliance with state privacy laws applicable in [STATE]
- [ ] If participant data includes education records, policy addresses FERPA requirements (20 U.S.C. 1232g)

### 3.2 Data Collection Consent

- [ ] Participant consent form is signed at enrollment, covering:
  - [ ] Collection and retention of personal information
  - [ ] Use of information for WIOA reporting
  - [ ] Follow-up contact for outcome tracking
  - [ ] Wage record matching (optional, with right to decline)
  - [ ] Sharing of information with referring agencies (LWDB, AJC) as needed for coordinated services
- [ ] Consent form clearly states participants may withdraw consent for follow-up contact at any time

### 3.3 Data Security

- [ ] Electronic participant records stored in password-protected files or systems
- [ ] Access to participant records restricted to authorized staff only
- [ ] Social Security Numbers stored only in the state workforce system and secured physical files (never in unencrypted spreadsheets or email)
- [ ] Physical participant files stored in a locked cabinet or secure room
- [ ] Staff with access to participant data have been trained on data privacy requirements
- [ ] Data breach response procedure established and documented

### 3.4 Data Retention and Destruction

- [ ] Records retention policy established: minimum [3 / 5 / 7] years after participant exit date (per state requirement, whichever is longer)
- [ ] Procedure for secure destruction of records after retention period expires (shredding for physical, secure deletion for electronic)
- [ ] Records will not be destroyed while any audit, litigation, or investigation is pending or anticipated

## 4. Financial Record Keeping

### 4.1 Financial Management System

- [ ] Financial management system that provides accurate, current, and complete disclosure of financial results for each WIOA-funded activity
- [ ] System identifies the source and application of WIOA funds
- [ ] Internal controls adequate to safeguard WIOA funds
- [ ] Comparison of actual expenditures to budgeted amounts

### 4.2 Cost Documentation

- [ ] All expenditures supported by source documentation (receipts, invoices, payroll records, contracts)
- [ ] Personnel costs supported by time and effort documentation showing allocation between WIOA and non-WIOA activities (if applicable)
- [ ] Cost allocation methodology documented if costs are shared between WIOA and other funding sources
- [ ] All costs charged to WIOA are allowable, allocable, reasonable, and necessary per 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)

### 4.3 Audit Requirements

- [ ] Determination made whether the organization is subject to Single Audit requirements (threshold: $750,000 in federal expenditures in a fiscal year)
- [ ] If subject to Single Audit: audit conducted annually by a qualified independent auditor in accordance with 2 CFR Part 200, Subpart F
- [ ] If below Single Audit threshold: financial records maintained in audit-ready condition for monitoring reviews
- [ ] Audit findings (if any) addressed with a corrective action plan within the required timeframe

### 4.4 Financial Reporting

- [ ] Quarterly financial reports submitted to the LWDB / state workforce agency on schedule
- [ ] Expenditure reports reconciled with participant enrollment and completion records
- [ ] Unexpended funds reported and disposition determined per agreement terms

## 5. Monitoring and Evaluation

### 5.1 Internal Monitoring

- [ ] Semi-annual internal review of participant files for completeness and accuracy
- [ ] Semi-annual review of data entry in state workforce system for consistency with source documents
- [ ] Quarterly review of performance data (completion rate, credential attainment, employment outcomes) against targets
- [ ] Documentation of internal monitoring activities and findings
- [ ] Corrective actions documented and implemented when deficiencies are identified

### 5.2 External Monitoring Cooperation

- [ ] Procedure for responding to monitoring visits from the LWDB, state workforce agency, or U.S. DOL
- [ ] Commitment to make all records available within [5] business days of a monitoring request
- [ ] Designated point of contact for monitoring inquiries
- [ ] Prior monitoring findings (if any) addressed and corrective actions completed

### 5.3 Continuous Improvement

- [ ] Participant satisfaction surveys administered after each cohort
- [ ] Survey results reviewed and analyzed quarterly
- [ ] Curriculum modifications documented with effective date and rationale
- [ ] Instructor performance evaluations conducted annually
- [ ] Performance trends analyzed and compared across cohorts

## 6. Grievance Procedures

### 6.1 Participant Grievance Policy

- [ ] Written grievance procedure for program participants, covering complaints related to:
  - [ ] Program quality or delivery
  - [ ] Discrimination or harassment
  - [ ] Denial of services or accommodations
  - [ ] Breach of confidentiality
  - [ ] Any other program-related concern
- [ ] Grievance procedure includes:
  - [ ] Multiple intake methods (written, verbal, electronic)
  - [ ] Designated individual to receive and investigate grievances
  - [ ] Timeline for acknowledgment (within [5] business days)
  - [ ] Timeline for resolution (within [30] business days)
  - [ ] Right to appeal within the organization
  - [ ] Information about filing complaints with external agencies (LWDB, state workforce agency, CRC at DOL)
- [ ] Grievance procedure provided to all participants at enrollment
- [ ] Grievance log maintained with: date received, nature of complaint, investigator, resolution, date resolved

### 6.2 Nondiscrimination Complaints

- [ ] Participants informed of their right to file discrimination complaints with:
  - [ ] The organization's Equal Opportunity Officer
  - [ ] The state WIOA Equal Opportunity Officer
  - [ ] The Director, Civil Rights Center (CRC), U.S. Department of Labor, 200 Constitution Avenue NW, Room N-4123, Washington, DC 20210
- [ ] Complaint must be filed within 180 days of the alleged discrimination (or 30 days after notification from the state if the state did not resolve the complaint)
- [ ] All discrimination complaints documented regardless of outcome

## 7. Participant Rights and Responsibilities

### 7.1 Participant Rights

- [ ] Written statement of participant rights provided at enrollment, including:
  - [ ] Right to receive training in a safe, respectful, and nondiscriminatory environment
  - [ ] Right to request and receive reasonable accommodations for disabilities
  - [ ] Right to access their own participant records
  - [ ] Right to privacy of personal information
  - [ ] Right to file a grievance without retaliation
  - [ ] Right to withdraw from the program at any time without penalty
  - [ ] Right to information about the program's performance outcomes

### 7.2 Participant Responsibilities

- [ ] Written statement of participant responsibilities provided at enrollment, including:
  - [ ] Attend scheduled sessions and communicate absences in advance
  - [ ] Complete assigned practice cases and homework
  - [ ] Participate actively in group activities and peer review
  - [ ] Provide accurate information on enrollment and follow-up forms
  - [ ] Comply with data privacy practices taught in the program
  - [ ] Respond to follow-up contacts for outcome tracking
  - [ ] Treat all participants, instructors, and staff with respect

### 7.3 Acknowledgment

- [ ] Participant acknowledgment of rights and responsibilities signed at enrollment and filed in participant record

## 10. Audit Readiness Checklist

The following items must be accessible and organized at all times for potential monitoring visits or audits:

### 10.1 Participant Files (for each WIOA-funded participant)

- [ ] Signed enrollment form with all required data elements
- [ ] WIOA eligibility determination documentation (or cross-reference to state system)
- [ ] Signed data privacy consent form
- [ ] Signed participant rights and responsibilities acknowledgment
- [ ] Attendance records (all 7 days)
- [ ] Pre-assessment results (Day 1)
- [ ] Post-assessment results (Day 7)
- [ ] Certification assessment results and score
- [ ] Certificate of completion or certificate of participation (copy)
- [ ] Follow-up contact log (all attempts and results)
- [ ] Employment and earnings verification documentation (survey responses, supporting docs)
- [ ] Any accommodation requests and documentation of accommodations provided
- [ ] Any grievance documentation (if applicable)

### 10.2 Program Files

- [ ] Current curriculum document
- [ ] Current certification assessment (with scoring rubric)
- [ ] Instructor qualifications documentation (resume, credentials, background check)
- [ ] Current nondiscrimination and EO policy
- [ ] Current grievance procedure
- [ ] Current privacy policy
- [ ] Current ADA accessibility plan
- [ ] LWDB training provider agreement (executed copy)
- [ ] Host site agreements (executed copies)
- [ ] Partnership agreements (executed copies)
- [ ] Certificate of insurance (general liability, with host sites as additional insured if required)
- [ ] Business registration and license documentation
- [ ] ETPL approval documentation

### 10.3 Financial Files

- [ ] General ledger / accounting records
- [ ] Bank statements and reconciliations
- [ ] All invoices submitted to LWDB with supporting documentation
- [ ] All payment receipts from LWDB
- [ ] Payroll records and tax filings
- [ ] Time and effort documentation for WIOA-charged personnel
- [ ] Cost allocation methodology (if applicable)
- [ ] Budget vs. actual expenditure comparison
- [ ] Single Audit report (if applicable)
