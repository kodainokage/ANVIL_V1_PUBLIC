## 1. Workforce Development Board Partnership Agreement

### TRAINING PROVIDER PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

**Between:**

[LOCAL WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT BOARD NAME] ("LWDB"), located at [ADDRESS]

**And:**

[ANVIL LEGAL ENTITY NAME], doing business as ANVIL -- AI Navigation & Vocational Integration Layer ("Training Provider"), located at [ADDRESS]

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**Agreement Period:** [START DATE] through [END DATE] (12 months, with option to renew)

---

#### 1.1 Purpose

This Agreement establishes the terms under which the Training Provider will deliver the ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Training Sprint to WIOA-eligible participants referred by the LWDB, and the terms under which the LWDB will fund participant training through WIOA Title I Adult and/or Dislocated Worker program funds.

#### 1.2 Training Provider Responsibilities

The Training Provider agrees to:

a) Deliver the ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Training Sprint in accordance with the program description, curriculum, and quality standards described in the program documentation on file with the LWDB.

b) Accept WIOA-eligible participants referred by the LWDB's American Job Center(s) in accordance with the enrollment procedures agreed upon by both parties.

c) Provide training at no cost to the participant.

d) Deliver training in cohorts of [10--25] participants, with new cohorts beginning approximately every [2 / 4] weeks, subject to sufficient enrollment.

e) Administer the ANVIL Certification Assessment and award the ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Certification to participants who meet completion requirements.

f) Track and report participant enrollment, attendance, completion, credential attainment, measurable skill gains, employment, and earnings outcomes in the format and on the schedule specified by the LWDB and/or state workforce agency.

g) Enter participant data into [STATE WORKFORCE SYSTEM NAME] within [5] business days of enrollment, completion, and exit events.

h) Maintain participant records for a minimum of [3 / 5 / 7] years in accordance with applicable federal and state record retention requirements.

i) Comply with all applicable federal, state, and local nondiscrimination laws and WIOA equal opportunity requirements.

j) Cooperate with monitoring, evaluation, and audit activities conducted by the LWDB, state workforce agency, or their designees.

k) Notify the LWDB within [10] business days of any material changes to the program, including changes to curriculum, instructor qualifications, delivery format, or location.

#### 1.3 LWDB Responsibilities

The LWDB agrees to:

a) Refer WIOA-eligible participants to the Training Provider through the American Job Center system.

b) Conduct WIOA eligibility determination for all referred participants prior to enrollment.

c) Issue Individual Training Accounts (ITAs) or authorize direct contract payments for enrolled participants at the rate of $[AMOUNT] per participant.

d) Process payment to the Training Provider within [30] days of receipt of a complete invoice and supporting documentation (enrollment verification, attendance records, and completion status).

e) Provide the Training Provider with access to [STATE WORKFORCE SYSTEM NAME] for participant data entry, or designate LWDB staff to enter data based on information provided by the Training Provider.

f) Assign a point of contact for the Training Provider to coordinate enrollment, reporting, and operational issues.

g) Notify the Training Provider of any changes to WIOA policies, performance standards, or reporting requirements that affect the program.

#### 1.4 Payment Terms

a) **Payment Rate:** $[AMOUNT] per enrolled participant.

b) **Payment Schedule:** [SELECT ONE]
   - [ ] Full payment upon participant completion
   - [ ] 50% upon enrollment, 50% upon completion
   - [ ] Monthly invoicing based on enrolled participants

c) **Invoice Submission:** The Training Provider will submit invoices to [LWDB CONTACT / ADDRESS] accompanied by:
   - Participant name and WIOA eligibility determination number
   - Enrollment date and cohort number
   - Attendance record
   - Completion status (completed / exited without completing)
   - Credential attainment status (if applicable at time of invoice)

d) **Non-completion:** For participants who exit the program without completing, payment will be [SELECT ONE]:
   - [ ] Pro-rated based on sessions attended (e.g., 3 of 7 sessions = 43% of full rate)
   - [ ] Not applicable (no payment for non-completers)
   - [ ] Paid at [X]% of full rate for participants attending minimum [3] sessions

#### 1.5 Performance Standards

The Training Provider agrees to meet the following minimum performance standards:

| Indicator | Minimum Standard |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Program Completion Rate | 70% |
| Credential Attainment Rate | 65% of completers |
| Employment Rate, Q2 After Exit | 50% |
| Median Earnings, Q2 After Exit | $5,000 |
| Measurable Skill Gains | 75% |

Performance will be evaluated annually. If performance falls below minimum standards for two consecutive quarters, the LWDB may require a performance improvement plan or terminate this Agreement in accordance with Section 1.8.

#### 1.6 Duration and Renewal

This Agreement is effective for 12 months from the Effective Date. It may be renewed for additional 12-month periods upon mutual written agreement of both parties and subject to continued satisfactory performance and availability of WIOA funds.

#### 1.7 Modifications

This Agreement may be modified only by written amendment signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

#### 1.8 Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement with [30 / 60] days written notice. In the event of termination:

a) The Training Provider will complete any cohort currently in progress.
b) The LWDB will pay for participants enrolled prior to the termination notice.
c) The Training Provider will continue to track and report outcomes for all enrolled participants through the required measurement periods.
d) Participant records will be retained in accordance with Section 1.2(h).

The LWDB may terminate this Agreement immediately if the Training Provider:
- Materially misrepresents program information or participant data
- Fails to comply with nondiscrimination requirements
- Is found to have committed fraud or financial mismanagement
- Loses any required business license, insurance, or authorization

## 2. Community College Host Site Agreement

### HOST SITE AGREEMENT FOR TRAINING PROGRAM DELIVERY

**Between:**

[COMMUNITY COLLEGE NAME] ("Host Site"), located at [ADDRESS]

**And:**

[ANVIL LEGAL ENTITY NAME], doing business as ANVIL ("Training Provider"), located at [ADDRESS]

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**Agreement Period:** [START DATE] through [END DATE]

---

#### 2.1 Purpose

This Agreement establishes the terms under which the Host Site will provide classroom space and designated resources for the Training Provider to deliver the ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Training Sprint to enrolled participants.

#### 2.2 Host Site Responsibilities

The Host Site agrees to provide:

a) **Classroom space** suitable for [10--25] participants, equipped with:
   - Seating and writing surfaces for each participant
   - Electrical outlets sufficient for participant laptop use
   - Wireless internet access capable of supporting [25+] simultaneous connections
   - A projection system or large display screen for instructor presentations
   - Climate control and lighting suitable for a full-day training session

b) **Facility access** during scheduled program hours, including [30] minutes before and after each session for setup and teardown. Scheduled hours: [DAYS AND TIMES].

c) **Restroom access** for all participants during program hours.

d) **Parking** or transit accessibility information for participants.

e) **On-site contact person** available during program hours to address facility issues (HVAC, internet connectivity, equipment malfunction, building access).

f) **Promotional support** (optional but appreciated): inclusion of the program in the Host Site's continuing education catalog, community bulletin boards, or website, subject to the Host Site's standard review and approval process.

#### 2.3 Training Provider Responsibilities

The Training Provider agrees to:

a) Deliver all instruction, curriculum materials, and assessment activities. The Host Site has no instructional or programmatic responsibility.

b) Provide advance notice of all scheduled cohort dates at least [14] days before each cohort begins.

c) Ensure all participants comply with the Host Site's facility rules, code of conduct, and campus policies during program hours.

d) Leave the classroom in the condition in which it was found after each session.

e) Maintain general liability insurance coverage of at least $[1,000,000] per occurrence, with the Host Site named as an additional insured on the policy. A certificate of insurance will be provided upon execution of this Agreement.

f) Indemnify and hold harmless the Host Site from claims arising out of the Training Provider's program activities, except for claims arising from the Host Site's own negligence.

g) Not use the Host Site's name, logo, or trademarks in promotional materials without prior written approval.

#### 2.4 Financial Terms

- [ ] **No charge.** The Host Site provides space at no cost as part of its community education and workforce development mission.
- [ ] **Facility fee.** The Training Provider will pay the Host Site $[AMOUNT] per cohort / per session day for classroom use.
- [ ] **Revenue share.** The Host Site will receive $[AMOUNT] per enrolled participant as a host site fee, payable within [30] days of cohort completion.
- [ ] **In-kind exchange.** The Training Provider will provide [DESCRIBE -- e.g., guest instruction in continuing education courses, free program slots for Host Site referrals, recognition in program materials].

#### 2.5 Duration and Termination

This Agreement is effective for the period stated above and may be renewed by mutual written agreement. Either party may terminate with [30] days written notice. In the event of termination, any cohort in progress will be completed at the Host Site unless both parties agree to an alternative arrangement.

## 3. Library System Partnership Agreement

### COMMUNITY PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

**Between:**

[LIBRARY SYSTEM NAME] ("Library"), located at [ADDRESS]

**And:**

[ANVIL LEGAL ENTITY NAME], doing business as ANVIL ("Training Provider"), located at [ADDRESS]

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**Agreement Period:** [START DATE] through [END DATE]

---

#### 3.1 Purpose

This Agreement establishes a partnership between the Library and the Training Provider to deliver free workforce development training to Library patrons and community members, using Library meeting room facilities and leveraging the Library's role as a trusted community resource.

#### 3.2 Scope of Partnership

The partnership encompasses:

a) **Training cohort delivery:** The Training Provider will deliver the ANVIL 7-day training program at Library branch location(s) to be mutually agreed upon. Anticipated schedule: [NUMBER] cohorts over [NUMBER] months, each lasting 7 consecutive days.

b) **Informational workshops:** The Training Provider may offer free 1-hour introductory workshops for Library patrons on topics including AI literacy, career transition strategies, and overview of the three ANVIL career pathways.

c) **Referral pathway:** Library staff may refer patrons who are job-seeking, underemployed, or facing benefit-related challenges to the Training Provider for enrollment consideration.

d) **Resource availability:** The Training Provider will provide printed or digital information about the program for display at Library branches.

#### 3.3 Library Responsibilities

The Library agrees to:

a) Provide meeting room space at [BRANCH NAME(S)] for scheduled training sessions, subject to the Library's standard room reservation policies and availability.

b) Allow the Training Provider to use the Library's wireless internet during program hours.

c) Display program information (flyers, brochures) in designated areas at participating branches.

d) Include program information in the Library's community events calendar and/or newsletter, subject to the Library's standard review and approval process.

e) Permit Library staff to refer interested patrons to the program (referral is voluntary and at the individual staff member's discretion).

f) Designate a Library staff contact for scheduling and logistical coordination.

#### 3.4 Training Provider Responsibilities

The Training Provider agrees to:

a) Deliver all instruction and program activities without cost to the Library or its patrons.

b) Comply with all Library policies regarding room use, noise levels, food and drink, and patron conduct.

c) Provide all necessary equipment and materials (the Library is not responsible for providing computers, projectors, or instructional materials beyond the meeting room space and internet access).

d) Schedule room use at least [14 / 30] days in advance through the Library's standard reservation process.

e) Not represent the program as endorsed by, operated by, or affiliated with the Library unless the Library provides explicit written authorization for such representation.

f) Maintain appropriate insurance coverage (general liability) during all program activities on Library premises.

g) Restrict program marketing on Library premises to approved display areas. The Training Provider will not approach or solicit individual Library patrons.

#### 3.5 Financial Terms

This partnership is non-financial. Neither party pays the other. Both parties contribute in-kind resources:

- **Library contribution:** Meeting room space, internet access, promotional support, referral pathway
- **Training Provider contribution:** Free training for Library patrons, community workshops, workforce development programming aligned with the Library's mission

#### 3.6 Intellectual Property and Branding

Neither party acquires any rights to the other party's name, logo, trademarks, or intellectual property. Use of either party's branding requires prior written approval.

#### 3.7 Duration and Termination

This Agreement is effective for the period stated above and may be renewed by mutual written agreement. Either party may terminate with [14] days written notice. Termination does not affect any cohort already in progress, which will be completed unless both parties agree otherwise.

## 4. Nonprofit Referral Partner Agreement

### REFERRAL PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

**Between:**

[NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION NAME] ("Referral Partner"), located at [ADDRESS]

**And:**

[ANVIL LEGAL ENTITY NAME], doing business as ANVIL ("Training Provider"), located at [ADDRESS]

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**Agreement Period:** [START DATE] through [END DATE]

---

#### 4.1 Purpose

This Agreement establishes a referral relationship between the Referral Partner, which serves individuals who may benefit from the ANVIL training program, and the Training Provider, which provides free workforce development training in AI-augmented specialist services.

#### 4.2 Referral Partner Responsibilities

The Referral Partner agrees to:

a) Identify clients, members, or program participants who may be appropriate candidates for the ANVIL training program based on the following criteria:
   - Adults (18+) who are unemployed, underemployed, or at risk of job loss
   - Individuals seeking career transition or skills development
   - Individuals with access to a computer and internet (or who can access these through Library or community resources)

b) Provide referred individuals with program information (provided by the Training Provider) and facilitate a warm introduction or direct referral.

c) Designate a staff contact for referral coordination.

d) Share anonymized, aggregated data on the number of referrals made (not individual client data) for partnership evaluation purposes.

#### 4.3 Training Provider Responsibilities

The Training Provider agrees to:

a) Accept referrals from the Referral Partner and prioritize enrollment for referred individuals, subject to available capacity.

b) Provide all training at no cost to referred individuals.

c) Provide the Referral Partner with program information materials (flyers, fact sheets, digital assets) for distribution to potential participants.

d) Share aggregate outcome data for referred participants (completion rate, credential attainment) with the Referral Partner, without identifying individual participants, for the purpose of partnership evaluation.

e) With participant consent, provide status updates to the Referral Partner's designated contact for individual referrals (e.g., "enrolled," "completed," "withdrew"), to facilitate coordinated service delivery.

f) Refer ANVIL participants who present needs outside the Training Provider's scope (housing, food, mental health, legal) to the Referral Partner or other appropriate community resources.

#### 4.4 Data Privacy

a) The Referral Partner will not share individual client records with the Training Provider without the client's informed written consent.

b) The Training Provider will not share individual participant records with the Referral Partner without the participant's informed written consent.

c) Both parties will comply with applicable privacy laws and organizational confidentiality policies.

d) Any status updates shared between the parties for coordinated service delivery require the participant's/client's prior written consent.

#### 4.5 Financial Terms

This referral partnership is non-financial. Neither party pays the other for referrals. Both parties engage in this partnership to better serve the populations they exist to help.

#### 4.6 Independence

This Agreement does not create an employment, agency, joint venture, or partnership relationship between the parties. Each party is an independent organization. Neither party has authority to bind the other.

#### 4.7 Duration and Termination

This Agreement is effective for the period stated above and may be renewed by mutual written agreement. Either party may terminate with [14] days written notice without cause.

## 5. American Job Center Collaboration Agreement

### COLLABORATION AGREEMENT FOR WORKFORCE TRAINING SERVICES

**Between:**

[AMERICAN JOB CENTER NAME / OPERATOR NAME] ("AJC"), located at [ADDRESS], operated by [OPERATOR ORGANIZATION NAME] under contract with [LOCAL WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT BOARD NAME]

**And:**

[ANVIL LEGAL ENTITY NAME], doing business as ANVIL ("Training Provider"), located at [ADDRESS]

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**Agreement Period:** [START DATE] through [END DATE]

---

#### 5.1 Purpose

This Agreement establishes the operational collaboration between the AJC and the Training Provider for the referral, enrollment, and support of WIOA-eligible individuals into the ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Training Sprint. This Agreement supplements and operates under the umbrella of the Training Provider Partnership Agreement between the Training Provider and the [LOCAL WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT BOARD NAME].

#### 5.2 AJC Responsibilities

The AJC agrees to:

a) **Eligibility Determination:** Conduct WIOA eligibility determination for individuals interested in the ANVIL program, including documentation of dislocated worker, low-income, or other qualifying status.

b) **Career Counseling:** Provide career counseling and assessment services to potential participants to confirm that the ANVIL program aligns with the individual's employment plan and career goals.

c) **ITA Authorization:** Issue Individual Training Account authorizations for eligible participants in accordance with LWDB policies and funding availability.

d) **Referral:** Refer eligible individuals to the Training Provider with a completed referral form that includes: participant name, contact information, WIOA eligibility determination number, career pathway interest, and any relevant case notes regarding barriers or support needs (with participant consent).

e) **Supportive Services:** Assess referred participants for WIOA supportive service needs (transportation, childcare, work-related supplies) and provide such services as authorized under the LWDB's supportive services policy.

f) **Co-enrollment:** Where appropriate, co-enroll ANVIL participants in other WIOA services (basic career services, individualized career services, follow-up services) to provide a comprehensive service package.

g) **Data Entry:** Enter participant enrollment, service, and outcome data into [STATE WORKFORCE SYSTEM NAME] in accordance with state data entry timelines and procedures, based on information provided by the Training Provider.

h) **Follow-up Services:** Provide WIOA follow-up services to ANVIL graduates for up to 12 months after program exit, as authorized under WIOA Section 134(c)(2)(A)(xiii).

#### 5.3 Training Provider Responsibilities

The Training Provider agrees to:

a) **Enrollment Confirmation:** Confirm enrollment of referred participants within [3] business days of the referral and provide enrollment documentation to the AJC contact.

b) **Progress Updates:** Provide attendance and progress updates to the AJC contact at the midpoint (Day 4) and conclusion (Day 7) of each cohort.

c) **Completion Notification:** Notify the AJC contact within [5] business days of a participant's completion or exit, including completion status, credential attainment, and any follow-up recommendations.

d) **Outcome Data:** Share employment and earnings follow-up data with the AJC for participants who consent to data sharing, to support WIOA performance reporting.

e) **Support Referrals:** Refer participants who present support needs (transportation, childcare, housing, mental health, legal) to the AJC for WIOA supportive services assessment.

f) **Coordination Meetings:** Participate in [monthly / quarterly] coordination meetings with AJC staff to review referral volume, enrollment outcomes, participant feedback, and operational improvements.

g) **Space for Outreach (if applicable):** If the AJC offers space for the Training Provider to conduct informational sessions or meet with potential participants on-site, the Training Provider will schedule such activities in advance and comply with AJC facility policies.

#### 5.4 Communication Protocol

| Event | Training Provider Action | Timeline |
|-------|------------------------|----------|
| Participant referred | Acknowledge receipt, confirm enrollment or waitlist status | Within 3 business days |
| Cohort begins | Confirm enrolled participants | Day 1 of cohort |
| Midpoint update | Send attendance and progress summary | Day 4 of cohort |
| Completion/exit | Send completion status and credential attainment | Within 5 business days of Day 7 |
| Follow-up data | Share employment/earnings survey results (with consent) | As collected (30, 90, 180 days, Q2, Q4) |
| Concerns or issues | Contact AJC point of contact | Within 24 hours |
