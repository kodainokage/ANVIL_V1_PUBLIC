# ANVIL Outcomes Measurement Framework

## Participant Outcomes Tracking and Reporting for WIOA Compliance

**Document Version:** 1.0

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

---

## 1. Overview

This document establishes the framework by which ANVIL tracks, measures, and reports participant outcomes to satisfy the performance accountability requirements of the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) Title I, Sections 116 and 122. The framework addresses all six WIOA Primary Indicators of Performance applicable to the Adult and Dislocated Worker programs, as well as supplemental metrics used for internal program improvement.

All data collection, storage, and reporting activities described herein comply with applicable federal and state privacy regulations, including the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) where applicable, and WIOA confidentiality requirements at 20 CFR 677.175.

---

## 2. WIOA Primary Indicators of Performance

ANVIL tracks the following indicators as required under WIOA Section 116(b)(2)(A):

| # | Indicator | Definition | Measurement Window |
|---|-----------|------------|--------------------|
| 1 | Employment Rate, 2nd Quarter After Exit | Percentage of participants in unsubsidized employment during the 2nd quarter after program exit | 4th--6th month after exit |
| 2 | Employment Rate, 4th Quarter After Exit | Percentage of participants in unsubsidized employment during the 4th quarter after program exit | 10th--12th month after exit |
| 3 | Median Earnings, 2nd Quarter After Exit | Median quarterly earnings of participants in unsubsidized employment during the 2nd quarter after exit | 4th--6th month after exit |
| 4 | Credential Attainment Rate | Percentage of participants who attain a recognized postsecondary credential or secondary school diploma during participation or within 1 year after exit | During program through 12 months after exit |
| 5 | Measurable Skill Gains | Percentage of participants in an education or training program who achieve documented skill gains during the program year | During program participation |
| 6 | Effectiveness in Serving Employers | As determined by the applicable state and Local Workforce Development Board methodology | Per state methodology |

## 4. Completion Tracking

### 4.1 Definition of "Completed"

A participant is classified as a **program completer** if they meet all of the following criteria:

1. Attended a minimum of 5 of 7 scheduled sessions
2. Completed a minimum of 3 practice cases (submitted for review)
3. Drafted a service agreement
4. Posted at least 1 client acquisition message on a live platform
5. Attempted the ANVIL Certification Assessment

A participant is classified as having **exited without completing** if they:
- Attended fewer than 5 sessions without documented make-up work, OR
- Withdrew voluntarily, OR
- Were removed from the program for cause

### 4.2 Attendance Tracking

Daily attendance is recorded by the instructor at the beginning of each session. Attendance records include:
- Participant name
- Session date
- Session number (Day 1--7)
- Attendance status (present, absent, late arrival with time noted)
- Make-up work status (if applicable)

Attendance records are maintained in the participant's file and in the aggregate cohort tracking spreadsheet.

### 4.3 Exit Date Determination

The **program exit date** is the last date of service, defined as:
- The date of the last attended session for participants who do not complete, OR
- Day 7 (the date of the certification assessment) for completers, OR
- The date of the retake assessment for participants who initially fail and retake within 30 days

The exit date determines the start of the WIOA follow-up period for employment and earnings measurement.

## 5. Credential Attainment Tracking

### 5.1 ANVIL AI-Augmented Specialist Certification

The ANVIL Certification is the program's primary credential. It is awarded based on a performance-based assessment administered on Day 7.

**Assessment Structure:**
- 10 questions covering practical competency domains
- Scored against a standardized rubric
- Passing score: 8/10
- One retake permitted within 30 days

**Credential Attainment is recorded when:**
- The participant passes the assessment with a score of 8/10 or higher
- The assessment date, score, and assessor name are documented

**Credential Attainment Rate is calculated as:**
Number of participants who attain the ANVIL Certification / Number of program completers who attempted the assessment x 100

### 5.2 Credential Documentation

Each certified graduate receives:
- A digital certificate (PDF) with the participant's name, certification date, career pathway, and a unique certificate number
- Entry in the ANVIL certified specialist directory (with participant consent)

Credential records are maintained indefinitely for verification purposes.

### 5.3 Additional Credentials

Participants who subsequently obtain related industry credentials (e.g., Certified Medical Reimbursement Specialist, National Association of Benefits and Work Incentives Specialists certification) within 1 year of program exit are recorded as additional credential attainments, provided the participant reports this information during follow-up.

## 6. Employment Placement Tracking

### 6.1 Definition of Employment

For WIOA reporting purposes, a participant is considered **employed** if, during the applicable quarter, they are in one or more of the following statuses:

- Employed by an employer (W-2 employment), verified through state wage records or participant self-report
- Self-employed and actively earning income through specialist services or related work, verified through participant self-report with supporting documentation (client invoices, payment records, tax filings)
- Enrolled in military service
- Enrolled in a registered apprenticeship

Note: Self-employment is a primary outcome pathway for ANVIL graduates. ANVIL follows DOL guidance on supplemental wage information (TEGL 26-15, Change 1) for self-employment verification where state wage records do not capture self-employment earnings.

### 6.2 Follow-Up Schedule

| Contact Point | Timing | Method | Purpose |
|--------------|--------|--------|---------|
| 30-day check-in | 30 days post-exit | Phone or email | Early engagement tracking, support needs |
| 60-day check-in | 60 days post-exit | Phone or email | Client activity, income status |
| Q2 follow-up | 4th month post-exit | Phone, email, and mail | WIOA employment and earnings (primary) |
| 90-day check-in | 90 days post-exit | Phone or email | Employment status, support referrals |
| 180-day check-in | 180 days post-exit | Phone, email, and mail | Sustained employment, practice viability |
| Q4 follow-up | 10th month post-exit | Phone, email, and mail | WIOA employment and earnings (primary) |

### 6.3 Data Collection Methods

**Primary Method: State Wage Record Match**
- With participant consent (obtained at enrollment via signed release), ANVIL requests wage record data from the applicable state workforce agency for Q2 and Q4 post-exit.
- Wage records capture W-2 employment and earnings but do not capture self-employment, independent contractor work, or informal employment.

**Supplementary Method: Participant Survey**
- ANVIL administers a structured follow-up survey at each contact point.
- The survey captures employment status, employment type (W-2, self-employment, contract), employer name and occupation (if applicable), hours worked, and earnings.
- For self-employed graduates, the survey requests copies or descriptions of client invoices, payment receipts, or tax records as supporting documentation.
- Survey responses are documented in the participant's file.

**Contact Protocol:**
- First attempt: Email with survey link
- Second attempt (if no response within 7 days): Phone call
- Third attempt (if no response within 14 days): Text message and/or mailed letter
- A minimum of 3 contact attempts are made for each follow-up point before recording the participant as "unable to contact."

### 6.4 Employment Rate Calculation

Employment Rate (Q2 or Q4) = Number of exiters employed in the applicable quarter / Number of exiters in the measurement cohort x 100

Participants who are "unable to contact" after 3 attempts are excluded from the denominator only if they represent less than 10% of the cohort. If more than 10% are non-responsive, they are included in the denominator as "not employed" for a conservative estimate.

## 7. Median Earnings Tracking

### 7.1 Earnings Data Sources

- **State wage records:** Capture gross quarterly earnings from W-2 employment
- **Participant self-report:** Capture gross quarterly earnings from self-employment, contract work, and other income sources not reflected in wage records
- Self-reported earnings are corroborated where possible through invoices, payment platform records (Venmo, Zelle, PayPal transaction histories), or tax filings (Schedule C)

### 7.2 Earnings Calculation

Median Earnings (Q2) = Median of all Q2 quarterly earnings amounts for participants who are employed in Q2

Only participants who are confirmed employed during the measurement quarter are included in the median earnings calculation. Participants who are employed but for whom earnings data is unavailable are excluded from the median calculation.

### 7.3 Self-Employment Earnings Documentation

ANVIL recognizes that self-employment earnings may be irregular during the ramp-up period. To capture accurate self-employment earnings:

- Graduates are instructed during the program to maintain records of all client engagements, fees charged, and payments received
- The follow-up survey includes specific fields for: number of clients served in the quarter, total fees charged, total payments received, and estimated hours worked
- Self-employment earnings are calculated as total payments received during the quarter, consistent with DOL guidance

## 8. Measurable Skill Gains

### 8.1 Definition

A Measurable Skill Gain (MSG) is documented academic, technical, occupational, or other form of progress toward a credential or employment, achieved during the program.

### 8.2 MSG Types Used by ANVIL

| MSG Type | How Measured | When Measured |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Skills progression (educational functioning level gain) | Pre/post skills assessment score improvement | Day 1 (pre) and Day 7 (post) |
| Skills progression (training milestone) | Completion of each program phase (Days 1-2, Days 3-4, Days 5-7) | Days 2, 4, and 7 |
| Satisfactory progress report | Instructor assessment of participant readiness at midpoint | Day 4 |

### 8.3 Pre/Post Skills Assessment

**Administered on Day 1 (before instruction) and Day 7 (before certification assessment):**

The skills assessment evaluates 5 domains on a 1--5 scale:

| Domain | Assessment Method |
|--------|------------------|
| AI Tool Proficiency | Practical task: use an AI tool to research a provided scenario and evaluate the output quality |
| Niche-Specific Knowledge | Written assessment: 10 questions about the participant's selected career pathway |
| Client Communication | Role-play evaluation: conduct a 5-minute simulated client interaction |
| Ethical Reasoning | Scenario-based assessment: respond to 3 ethical boundary scenarios |
| Quality Assurance | Document review: identify errors in a provided sample deliverable |

A participant demonstrates a Measurable Skill Gain if their total post-assessment score exceeds their pre-assessment score by 3 or more points (out of 25 total).

### 8.4 MSG Rate Calculation

MSG Rate = Number of participants with documented MSG / Number of participants enrolled in the program year x 100

## 9. Client Satisfaction Surveys

### 9.1 Post-Program Survey (Administered Day 7 or Day 8)

All participants, regardless of completion status, are invited to complete a post-program satisfaction survey.

**Survey Domains:**

| Domain | Questions | Scale |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Overall satisfaction | "How satisfied are you with the ANVIL program?" | 1--5 (Very Dissatisfied to Very Satisfied) |
| Instructor quality | "How would you rate the instructor's knowledge and teaching ability?" | 1--5 |
| Curriculum relevance | "How relevant was the curriculum to your career goals?" | 1--5 |
| Practical preparation | "How prepared do you feel to serve your first client?" | 1--5 |
| Would recommend | "How likely are you to recommend this program to someone in a similar situation?" | 1--10 (Net Promoter Score) |
| Open-ended | "What was the most valuable part of the program?" | Free text |
| Open-ended | "What would you change about the program?" | Free text |
| Open-ended | "Is there anything the program did not cover that you wish it had?" | Free text |

### 9.2 Follow-Up Satisfaction Check

At the 90-day follow-up, graduates are asked:

- "Looking back, how would you rate the value of the ANVIL program?" (1--5)
- "Has the training helped you earn income or find employment?" (Yes/No + details)
- "What additional support would be most helpful to you right now?" (Free text)

## 12. Data Security and Privacy

### 12.1 Data Storage

- All participant records are stored in password-protected electronic files with access restricted to authorized program staff.
- Physical records (signed enrollment forms, consent forms) are stored in a locked filing cabinet at [PROGRAM OFFICE ADDRESS].
- Social Security Numbers are stored only in the state workforce system and in the participant's physical enrollment file. They are not stored in program spreadsheets or unsecured electronic files.

### 12.2 Data Access

- Access to participant data is limited to: program director, program administrator, instructors (attendance and assessment data only), and authorized state/LWDB monitoring staff.
- Participant data is never shared with third parties without written participant consent, except as required by law or as necessary for WIOA reporting to the state workforce agency.

### 12.3 Participant Consent

At enrollment, each participant signs a consent form authorizing:
- Collection and retention of personal information for program administration and WIOA reporting
- Contact for follow-up surveys at 30, 60, 90, 180, and 365 days post-exit
- Wage record matching through the state workforce agency (optional; participant may decline)
- Use of anonymized, aggregated data in program performance reports

Participants may withdraw consent for follow-up contact at any time without affecting their program participation or certification status.

### 12.4 Data Retention

Participant records are retained for a minimum of [3 / 5 / 7] years after the participant's program exit date, or longer if required by the applicable state retention schedule or if any audit or litigation is pending.

## 13. Quality Assurance for Data Integrity

### 13.1 Data Verification Procedures

- Enrollment data is reviewed for completeness within 5 business days of entry.
- Assessment scores are verified by a second reviewer for any participant scoring at the pass/fail boundary (7/10 or 8/10).
- Follow-up survey responses are reviewed for internal consistency (e.g., a respondent reporting "not employed" while also reporting quarterly earnings is contacted for clarification).
- Self-reported employment and earnings data is corroborated where possible through wage records, invoices, or payment platform documentation.

### 13.2 Internal Audits

The program director conducts a semi-annual review of:
- Completeness of participant files (enrollment form, attendance records, assessment results, follow-up documentation)
- Accuracy of quarterly report calculations
- Follow-up contact attempt documentation
- Consistency between individual files and aggregate data

### 13.3 Monitoring Cooperation

ANVIL cooperates fully with monitoring visits and data reviews conducted by:
- The applicable Local Workforce Development Board
- The state workforce agency
- The U.S. Department of Labor (for federal monitoring)
- Independent auditors engaged by any of the above

All requested records will be made available within 5 business days of a monitoring request.
