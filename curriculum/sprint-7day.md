### Session 1A: What AI Can and Can't Do (30 min)

**AI CAN:**
- Research government programs and eligibility criteria faster than any human
- Analyze medical bills line by line for errors, duplicates, and overcharges
- Draft appeal letters, applications, and dispute documents
- Summarize complex regulations into plain language
- Compare costs, programs, and options across states
- Find information buried in government websites that would take hours to locate manually
- Generate checklists, templates, and structured workflows

**AI CANNOT:**
- Give legal advice (and you should never present your work as legal advice)
- Give medical advice or diagnose conditions
- Make financial investment recommendations
- Access private databases, medical records, or government systems directly
- Replace the human relationship — empathy, trust, hand-holding through scary processes
- Guarantee accuracy — you MUST verify everything AI produces

**AI SOMETIMES GETS WRONG (and you must always check):**
- Specific dollar amounts for benefits (these change annually and vary by state)
- Whether a specific program is currently accepting applications
- Exact eligibility thresholds (always verify against the official source)
- Links and URLs (AI frequently generates plausible but broken links)
- Legal citations and regulation numbers

**THE GOLDEN RULE:** AI is your research assistant, not your authority. Every output must be verified against an official source before you share it with a client. Your value is the verification, the human explanation, and the handholding — not the raw AI output.

### Session 2C: The Service Delivery Model (45 min)

Every ANVIL niche follows the same four-step delivery model:

**Step 1: INTAKE (15-30 min with client)**
- Collect basic information: location, household size, income, situation
- Understand what they need and what they've already tried
- Set expectations: what you will and will not do
- Get written consent to proceed (template provided)

**Step 2: AI-ASSISTED ANALYSIS (30-90 min, done independently)**
- Run your niche-specific AI prompts with the client's information
- Cross-check AI outputs against official sources
- Build your deliverable: a clear, organized document the client can use

**Step 3: HUMAN REVIEW (15-30 min)**
- Verify every claim, link, dollar amount, and eligibility threshold
- Check all links actually work
- Remove anything you're not confident about
- Add your own notes, explanations, and recommendations

**Step 4: CLIENT DELIVERY (30-45 min with client)**
- Walk the client through your findings in plain language
- Answer their questions
- Help them understand next steps (what to do, in what order)
- Provide the written deliverable (PDF or printed)
- Offer follow-up support (included in your fee or as an add-on)

**Time investment per case:**
- Simple case (benefits check): 1.5-2 hours total
- Medium case (bill audit): 2-3 hours total
- Complex case (disability application): 3-5 hours total

### Session 3A: Prompt Engineering Fundamentals (30 min)

The quality of your AI output is directly proportional to the quality of your prompts. These five principles will make every prompt you write better.

**Principle 1: Assign a Role**
Bad: "Look at this medical bill."
Good: "You are a medical billing analyst with 20 years of experience reviewing hospital bills for errors."

**Principle 2: Provide Context**
Bad: "Find benefits for this person."
Good: "I'm helping a client navigate government benefits. They are a 45-year-old single mother of two in Florida, recently laid off from a retail management position. Household income dropped from $52,000/year to $14,000/year (unemployment benefits). No health insurance. Here is their information: [...]"

**Principle 3: Be Specific About What You Want**
Bad: "What benefits can they get?"
Good: "List every federal, state, and Florida-specific benefit program this person may be eligible for. For each program, include: program name, estimated monthly benefit, eligibility requirements, application URL, and any application deadlines."

**Principle 4: Specify the Output Format**
Bad: "Give me the results."
Good: "Present your findings as a table with columns: Program Name | Estimated Monthly Benefit | Key Eligibility Requirement | How to Apply (direct URL) | Application Deadline. Sort by estimated benefit amount, highest first."

**Principle 5: Iterate and Refine**
Your first prompt rarely gives the best output. Follow up:
- "For item #3, provide more detail about the eligibility requirements."
- "Are there any programs you missed that are specific to [COUNTY]?"
- "Double-check the income thresholds — are those 2025 or 2026 numbers?"
- "The SNAP link you provided doesn't work. Give me the correct application URL for [STATE]."

### Session 4A: What You CAN and CANNOT Do (45 min)

This is the most important session of the entire sprint. Getting this wrong can expose you to legal liability. Getting it right makes you trustworthy and referable.

**YOU CAN (across all niches):**
- Research programs, eligibility criteria, and application processes
- Help clients understand their options in plain language
- Assist with paperwork and application completion (as directed by the client)
- Draft letters, appeals, and communications (for client review and signature)
- Organize and present information in a clear, actionable format
- Refer clients to appropriate licensed professionals when needed
- Charge for your time, research, and document preparation

**YOU CANNOT (across all niches):**
- Provide legal advice or represent yourself as an attorney
- Provide medical advice or diagnose conditions
- Provide financial advice or recommend specific investments
- Guarantee outcomes ("You WILL get approved" — never say this)
- Sign documents on behalf of a client
- Access client accounts or systems with their credentials
- Make decisions for a client — you present options, they decide
- Practice any activity that requires a professional license in your state

**THE LANGUAGE THAT PROTECTS YOU:**
- Say: "Based on my research, you appear to be eligible for..."
- Don't say: "You qualify for..."
- Say: "This letter template is for your review — please read it carefully before signing."
- Don't say: "Sign this and send it."
- Say: "I recommend consulting with a [lawyer/doctor/financial advisor] about this specific aspect."
- Don't say: "You don't need a lawyer for this."

**NICHE-SPECIFIC BOUNDARIES:**

*Benefits Navigation:* You can research eligibility and help with applications. You cannot guarantee approval or represent clients in hearings.

*Medical Bill Auditing:* You can identify potential errors and draft dispute letters. You cannot provide medical opinions on whether a treatment was necessary.

*Insurance Appeals:* You can draft appeal letters and compile supporting documentation. You cannot provide legal representation or medical opinions.

*Senior Services:* You can research programs and assist with applications. You cannot provide medical, legal, or financial advice specific to their situation.

*Small Business Compliance:* You can research applicable regulations and organize compliance checklists. You cannot provide legal advice or certify compliance.

*Tenant Rights:* You can research tenant rights and draft letters. You cannot provide legal representation in court proceedings.

*Disability Benefits:* You can help organize documentation and draft applications. You cannot provide medical opinions or guarantee outcomes. (Note: In most states, non-attorney disability representatives must register with SSA.)

### Session 4B: Quality Assurance Checklist (30 min)

Every deliverable you produce must pass this checklist before it reaches a client.

**The ANVIL Quality Checklist:**

- [ ] All dollar amounts verified against official 2026 sources
- [ ] All eligibility thresholds confirmed for the client's state and county
- [ ] All links tested and working (clicked, not just read)
- [ ] All program names are current (programs get renamed, merged, and discontinued)
- [ ] No AI hallucinations present (fabricated programs, wrong agencies, fake URLs)
- [ ] Language is plain and clear — a high schooler could understand it
- [ ] Appropriate disclaimers included (not legal/medical/financial advice)
- [ ] No guaranteed outcomes promised anywhere in the document
- [ ] Client's personal information is handled appropriately
- [ ] Next steps are specific and actionable ("Call this number" not "contact them")
- [ ] Your contact information is included for follow-up questions
- [ ] Document is formatted cleanly (no raw AI output, no prompt artifacts)
- [ ] You have noted anything you are uncertain about and flagged it for the client

**Print this checklist. Tape it next to your screen. Use it every single time.**

### Session 4C: Data Privacy and Client Confidentiality (30 min)

You will handle sensitive personal information. Treat it like a doctor treats medical records.

**Rules:**

1. **Never paste real client data into AI tools without consent.** Explain to clients that you use AI research tools and get their written permission. Many clients won't care — but they deserve to know.

2. **Anonymize when possible.** Instead of "John Smith, SSN 123-45-6789, 742 Oak Street," use "Client, age 58, income $24,000, household of 2, [STATE]." You rarely need identifying details in your AI prompts.

3. **Secure your files.** Client documents should be in a password-protected folder or encrypted drive. Not in your regular downloads folder. Not in a shared Google Drive.

4. **Delete when done.** After a case is closed, retain only what you need for your records. Delete AI conversation histories that contain client data.

5. **Never share client information** with anyone without explicit consent — including in cohort discussions, social media posts, or marketing materials.

6. **Clear your AI chat history** after each client session. Most AI tools allow you to delete conversations.

### Session 4D: Ethical Scenario Exercise (30 min)

**Work through these 5 scenarios. For each one, write: What would you do? What would you say to the client? Where is the ethical boundary?**

**Scenario 1:** A client asks you to help them apply for disability benefits. During your research, you notice that their described symptoms don't match common eligibility criteria. The client says, "Just write it so I'll get approved."

**Scenario 2:** You're auditing a medical bill and you find what appears to be a significant error — but you're not 100% sure. The client is eager and wants to call the hospital immediately.

**Scenario 3:** A client shares their Social Security number, bank account information, and Medicare login credentials with you, saying "just do whatever you need to do."

**Scenario 4:** You help a client find $3,200 in unclaimed benefits. They're thrilled and post on Facebook recommending you, calling you a "benefits lawyer." You see the post.

**Scenario 5:** A friend asks you to "just quickly look up" whether their elderly mother qualifies for a specific program. No intake form, no consent, no formal engagement.

**Discuss your answers with your cohort or peer.** There are no trick answers — but there are clearly right and wrong approaches. The key themes: don't guarantee outcomes, don't practice outside your scope, always get consent, always be transparent.

### Session 5B: Writing Posts That Attract (Not Repel) (45 min)

The #1 mistake new service providers make: they sound like they're selling something. Your posts must sound like a neighbor offering help, because that's exactly what you are.

**The Formula:**
1. Lead with the PROBLEM (not your service)
2. Offer something FREE (eligibility check, bill review, etc.)
3. Be specific about what you'll do
4. Remove all risk ("no strings attached," "no obligation")
5. Make it easy to respond ("DM me" or "comment below")

**Bad example:**
"Hi! I'm a certified AI-powered benefits navigation specialist offering comprehensive eligibility assessments. Visit my website to learn more about my services and book a consultation. Packages starting at $75."

**Good example:**
"Hey neighbors — did you know there are government programs that help with heating bills, internet costs, and prescription drugs that most people don't know about? I recently trained as a benefits navigator and I'm offering free eligibility checks for anyone in [AREA]. I'll look up what you might qualify for and send you a simple list with links to apply. No cost, no strings. DM me if you want me to check for you."

### Session 5D: The "Free First Case" Strategy (30 min)

Your first 3 clients should be free. This is not charity — this is your investment in testimonials, referrals, and confidence.

**Why free works:**
- Removes all risk for the client (they have nothing to lose)
- Gives you real-world practice without the pressure of a paid engagement
- Generates testimonials and word-of-mouth referrals
- Builds your case portfolio
- Free clients almost always refer you to paying clients

**The script:**
"I'm a benefits navigator [or your niche] and I'm looking for a few people to work with this week at no charge as I build my practice. I'll do a complete [DELIVERABLE] for you — same quality I'll charge for later — and all I ask in return is an honest testimonial if you're happy with the work. Interested?"

**Who to offer free cases to:**
- The people who respond to your posts
- Friends or family members who have the problem you solve
- Anyone referred to you by a community organization
- People you meet at community events

**Transition to paid:** After your 3 free cases, your script changes: "My standard rate is $[AMOUNT], but I'm offering a 50% discount for the first [5] clients." Then: full price with confidence.

### Session 6B: Peer Review (30 min)

**Swap your deliverable with a cohort peer.** (If no peer is available, use the AI peer review prompt below.)

**Peer review checklist:**
- Is the document clear enough for a non-expert to understand and act on?
- Are the next steps specific? ("Call 1-800-XXX-XXXX and reference case number Y" vs. "Contact them")
- Are there any claims that seem unverified or potentially inaccurate?
- Does the document make any promises or guarantees?
- Is the formatting professional and consistent?
- Would you feel confident if a client showed this to a lawyer or social worker?

**AI peer review prompt (if no human peer available):**

```
You are a quality assurance reviewer for a [NICHE] service. I'm going to paste a client deliverable I produced. Review it critically for:

1. Clarity: Could a non-expert understand and act on this?
2. Accuracy concerns: Are there any claims that seem unverified or potentially incorrect?
3. Professionalism: Is the formatting clean and consistent?
4. Scope: Does anything cross the line into legal/medical/financial advice?
5. Actionability: Are next steps specific enough for the client to follow without further help?
6. Missing items: Is there anything obvious that should be included but isn't?

Be harsh. I'd rather fix problems now than have a client find them.
```

**Revise your deliverable** based on feedback. Save both versions (before and after review) in your portfolio.

### Session 6C: Client Communication Scripts (30 min)

**The Expectation-Setting Call (before you start work):**

"Hi [CLIENT NAME], thanks for reaching out. Here's how this works: I'm going to research your situation and put together a clear document showing [what you're delivering]. This usually takes me [TIME ESTIMATE]. A few important things upfront — I'm a trained [NICHE] specialist, but I'm not a lawyer [or doctor, etc.]. I research and organize information to help you make informed decisions, but I always recommend consulting with a licensed professional for anything complex. I'll have your results ready by [DATE]. Any questions before I get started?"

**The Delivery Call (when presenting results):**

"Hi [CLIENT NAME], I've completed your [DELIVERABLE]. Let me walk you through what I found. [Walk through document section by section in plain language.] The most important takeaway is [KEY FINDING]. Your next step is [SPECIFIC ACTION]. I've included everything in this document, which I'll email/text you right after this call. Do you have any questions about anything I've covered?"

**The Follow-Up (3-5 days after delivery):**

"Hi [CLIENT NAME], just checking in — were you able to [NEXT STEP from your deliverable]? If you ran into any issues or have questions, I'm here to help. Also, if you know anyone else who could use this kind of help, I'd really appreciate a referral. Thanks again for trusting me with this."

**Practice each script out loud.** Not in your head — out loud. The first time you say these words should not be with a real client.

### Session 6D: Pricing Your Services (30 min)

**Do not underprice yourself.** The most common mistake new service providers make is charging too little. This signals low quality, attracts clients who don't value your work, and burns you out.

**Pricing models by niche:**

| Model | How It Works | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Per case/project | Flat fee for a defined deliverable | Benefits navigation, disability |
| Hourly | Bill by the hour with a time estimate upfront | Senior services, compliance |
| Percentage of savings | Charge 25-33% of what you save the client | Medical bill auditing |
| Retainer | Monthly fee for ongoing support | Small business compliance |

**Recommended starting prices (adjust for your market):**

| Niche | Starting Price | Move to After 10 Clients |
|-------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Benefits Navigation | $65-85 per case | $85-125 per case |
| Medical Bill Auditing | 25% of savings (min $75) | 30% of savings (min $100) |
| Insurance Appeals | $100-150 per appeal | $150-250 per appeal |
| Senior Services | $65-85/hr | $85-120/hr |
| Small Business Compliance | $85-100/hr | $100-150/hr |
| Tenant Rights | $60-80 per case | $80-120 per case |
| Disability Benefits | $125-175 per case | $175-250 per case |

**The confidence script:** When a potential client asks "how much," don't apologize, don't hedge, don't say "well, um, I was thinking maybe..." Say this:

"My fee for a [DELIVERABLE] is [AMOUNT]. That includes [WHAT'S INCLUDED]. Most clients find that the value I deliver is [X] times what they pay — the average person I work with [DISCOVERS $X IN BENEFITS / SAVES $X ON THEIR BILL / etc.]. Would you like to get started?"

**Sliding scale policy (recommended):** "My standard rate is $[X]. If that's outside your budget, let me know and we'll figure something out." This shows that you value your work AND that you're human. Never apologize for your prices.

### Session 7A: Certification Assessment (45 min)

**The ANVIL Practical Assessment — 10 questions.**

This is not a memorization test. It is a practical evaluation of your ability to deliver your service safely, ethically, and effectively.

**Format:** You receive a case file and 10 questions covering:

1. **Intake:** Given this client situation, what information do you still need before starting?
2. **Scope:** Which of these 4 requests from the client are within your scope, and which are not?
3. **AI workflow:** Write the prompt you would use to begin researching this case.
4. **Verification:** The AI produced this output. Identify 2 items that need verification and explain how you would verify them.
5. **Ethics:** The client asks you to [ethical boundary scenario]. How do you respond?
6. **Quality:** Review this sample deliverable. Identify 3 issues that should be fixed before sending to the client.
7. **Communication:** Write the 2-sentence opening you would use to present these findings to the client.
8. **Referral:** At what point in this case should you refer the client to a licensed professional, and which type?
9. **Privacy:** The client sent you a photo of their Social Security card "for your records." What do you do?
10. **Pricing:** The client asks about your fees. Write your exact response.

**Passing score:** 8/10. You have one retry if needed.

**This assessment protects you, your clients, and the ANVIL community.** Take it seriously.
