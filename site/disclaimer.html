Professional Services Disclaimer

This page contains critical disclaimers about what ANVIL is and what it is not. Please read this carefully before using our platform or engaging with any ANVIL-trained specialist. This disclaimer protects both you and our specialists.

ANVIL does not provide legal advice, medical advice, financial advice, or tax advice. ANVIL-trained specialists are not licensed professionals. If you need professional advice, consult a licensed attorney, physician, CPA, or financial advisor.

1. Not Professional Advice

ANVIL is a training platform. We teach people to become AI-augmented specialists who help others navigate complex systems. Here is what that means and what it does not mean:

ANVIL Specialists Are NOT:

  • Licensed attorneys or legal professionals of any kind
  • Licensed physicians, nurses, or healthcare providers
  • Certified Public Accountants (CPAs) or licensed tax preparers
  • Licensed financial advisors, investment advisors, or fiduciaries
  • Licensed insurance agents or brokers
  • Licensed social workers (unless separately credentialed)
  • Government employees or representatives of any government agency

ANVIL Specialists ARE:

  • Trained researchers who use AI tools to find publicly available information
  • Navigators who help people understand their options and next steps
  • Advocates who assist with paperwork, phone calls, and process management
  • Educators who explain how systems work in plain language

What This Means for You

  • Information provided by ANVIL specialists is for educational and research purposes only.
  • Nothing provided by an ANVIL specialist constitutes legal, medical, financial, or tax advice.
  • You should always consult a licensed professional before making important decisions about legal matters, medical treatment, financial planning, or tax obligations.
  • No attorney-client relationship, doctor-patient relationship, or advisor-client relationship is created by interacting with ANVIL or any ANVIL-trained specialist.

2. AI Tool Disclaimer

ANVIL trains specialists to use artificial intelligence tools as research assistants. Here is what you need to know about how AI is used:

How AI Tools Are Used

  • ANVIL specialists use AI tools (such as ChatGPT, Claude, and similar systems) to research eligibility requirements, regulations, billing codes, and other publicly available information.
  • AI tools are used as research assistants, not as decision-makers.
  • All AI-generated output is reviewed and verified by the human specialist before being shared with a client.
  • AI tools help specialists work faster and more thoroughly -- they do not replace human judgment.

Limitations of AI

  • AI can make mistakes. AI tools can produce incorrect, outdated, or incomplete information. This is why human review is a core part of the ANVIL methodology.
  • AI is not a substitute for professional advice. An AI tool cannot understand your complete personal situation, and its output should not be treated as professional guidance.
  • AI tools are provided by third parties. ANVIL does not control the AI models used by specialists. The accuracy, availability, and terms of use of these tools are determined by their respective providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.).
  • All information should be independently verified. Before acting on any information obtained with the help of AI tools, verify it with the relevant government agency, healthcare provider, or professional.

3. Service-Specific Disclaimers

Each ANVIL service niche has specific boundaries. Read the disclaimer for the service you are using or providing:

Benefits Navigation

ANVIL benefits navigation specialists help people understand what government programs they may be eligible for (such as SSDI, SSI, SNAP, Medicaid, Medicare, TANF, and others). Important limitations:

  • Specialists are not licensed enrollment counselors or certified application counselors.
  • Information about program eligibility is educational only. Actual eligibility is determined solely by the program administrator (Social Security Administration, state Medicaid office, etc.).
  • Specialists may help with paperwork and applications, but cannot guarantee approval or any specific outcome.
  • Program rules, income thresholds, and eligibility criteria change frequently. Information may not reflect the most current requirements.
  • Specialists cannot represent clients in administrative hearings or legal proceedings.

Medical Billing Advocacy

ANVIL medical billing specialists help people review medical bills for potential errors and understand their billing statements. Important limitations:

  • Bill review and auditing is not medical advice and is not legal advice.
  • Identifying potential billing errors is an educational service. The specialist is pointing out possible discrepancies for the client to review and take action on.
  • Specialists are not a substitute for a healthcare attorney. If you believe you have been a victim of medical billing fraud or need to pursue legal action against a provider, consult a licensed attorney.
  • Specialists cannot diagnose medical conditions, interpret clinical information, or make medical recommendations.
  • Negotiation with providers is conducted on the client's behalf as an advocate, not as a legal representative.
  • Results of billing disputes depend on the provider, insurer, and specific circumstances. No specific outcome is guaranteed.

Small Business Compliance

ANVIL compliance specialists help small business owners understand their regulatory obligations and maintain compliance. Important limitations:

  • Compliance guidance is educational only and is not legal advice or tax advice.
  • Specialists are not business attorneys, CPAs, or licensed tax professionals.
  • Regulatory requirements vary by state, county, city, and industry. Specialists provide general guidance, not jurisdiction-specific legal opinions.
  • For your specific business situation, consult a licensed business attorney and a CPA.
  • Compliance information may not reflect the most recent regulatory changes. Always verify current requirements with the relevant regulatory agency.
  • Specialists cannot file legal documents, tax returns, or regulatory filings on your behalf unless explicitly authorized and legally permitted.

Estate Document Preparation

ANVIL estate document specialists assist with preparing wills, trusts, powers of attorney, and other estate planning documents. Important limitations:

  • Document preparation is not the practice of law. Specialists fill in forms and templates based on information provided by the client.
  • Specialists are not estate planning attorneys and cannot provide legal advice about estate planning strategies.
  • Documents prepared by specialists should be reviewed by a licensed attorney before execution, especially for complex estates.
  • State laws governing wills, trusts, and estate documents vary significantly. Compliance with your state's specific requirements is the client's responsibility.

Grant Writing

ANVIL grant writing specialists help nonprofits and organizations prepare funding proposals. Important limitations:

  • Grant writing assistance does not guarantee funding approval.
  • Funding decisions are made solely by the granting organization based on their criteria and available funds.
  • Specialists are not financial auditors and do not verify the financial statements or organizational health of the applicant.

Insurance Claims Help

ANVIL insurance claims specialists help individuals navigate insurance claim denials and appeals. Important limitations:

  • Claims assistance is not legal representation. Specialists cannot represent clients in legal proceedings against insurance companies.
  • Specialists are not licensed insurance agents or adjusters.
  • If your claim involves bad faith, fraud, or requires legal action, consult a licensed insurance attorney.
  • Appeal outcomes depend on the insurer, policy terms, and specific circumstances. No specific outcome is guaranteed.

Debt Negotiation

ANVIL debt negotiation specialists help individuals communicate with creditors to resolve outstanding debts. Important limitations:

  • Debt negotiation is not legal advice and is not financial advice.
  • Specialists are not licensed debt counselors, attorneys, or financial advisors.
  • Debt settlement can have tax implications (forgiven debt may be taxable income). Consult a CPA or tax professional.
  • Debt negotiation can affect your credit score. Specialists cannot predict or guarantee specific credit impacts.
  • If you are considering bankruptcy, consult a licensed bankruptcy attorney.
  • No specific debt reduction amount or settlement outcome is guaranteed.
