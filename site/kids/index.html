AI Safety

Your child never gets raw AI.

Every AI interaction goes through guided, fill-in-the-blank templates. No open-ended prompts. No data harvesting. Full parental visibility.

Guided Prompts Only

Kids never type raw prompts into AI. Every interaction uses structured, fill-in-the-blank templates designed by educators. The AI assists — it never leads.

No Data Collection

COPPA compliant. We collect minimal PII — display name and age group only. No email required for children. No tracking. No ad targeting. Ever.

Parent Dashboard

See everything your child does. Track progress, review artifacts, monitor activity logs, and control which paths they access. You stay in charge.

No Social Features

Children under 13 have no public profiles, no chat, no social feeds. Portfolio sharing is opt-in and parent-controlled. Safety is the default.

FAQ

Questions parents ask

Yes. ANVIL Kids is fully COPPA compliant. We collect minimal personal information (display name and age group only), require verifiable parental consent before any child account is created, and never share data with third parties. Children under 13 have no public profiles and no social features. All AI interactions use guided templates, not open-ended prompts.

ANVIL Kids is designed for ages 8-18, split into two groups. Explorer paths (ages 8-12) feature simpler language, more visual guidance, and shorter quests. Builder paths (ages 13-18) tackle more complex topics like data analysis, grant writing, and AI ethics with greater independence. Both groups produce real portfolio artifacts.

Yes. One subscription covers up to 5 child profiles. Each child gets their own progress tracking, XP, level, path recommendations, and portfolio. You manage all profiles from a single parent dashboard. No extra charge per child.

If your child is 12-13 and falls between groups, start with the quiz — it considers maturity and interest level, not just age. You can assign either Explorer or Builder when creating their profile, and switch anytime. Many 12-year-olds thrive on Builder paths, while some 13-year-olds prefer starting with Explorer content first.

Instead of typing open-ended prompts, children fill in structured templates. For example: "I am researching [topic]. Help me find 3 facts about [specific question] from [source type]." The AI sees only the completed template, never raw free-form input. This prevents misuse, keeps outputs educational, and teaches kids how to give clear instructions — a professional AI skill in itself.

Yes. Cancel anytime from your account settings. On monthly plans, you keep access through the end of your current billing period. On annual plans, you keep access through the end of your year. All portfolio artifacts your child created are always accessible, even after cancellation.

No. Children do not need an email address. The parent creates child profiles using only a display name and age group. All authentication goes through the parent account. This is by design — we collect as little personal information as possible.

When a child completes all 20 quests in a path, they earn a completion badge and their final portfolio artifact is marked as a capstone project. They can start any other path immediately — there is no lock between paths. The portfolio grows with each completed path, building a tangible record of skills they can reference for school applications, internships, or simply pride.
