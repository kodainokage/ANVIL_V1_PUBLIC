# Government Benefits Navigation Playbook
## ANVIL Workforce Services — Practitioner Guide v1.0

**Purpose:** This playbook enables a trained benefits navigator to assess client eligibility for 20+ federal and state programs, prepare applications, and maximize total benefit recovery using AI-assisted workflows.

**Time to first client:** Same day. Read the intake script, run the AI prompt, and walk them through the first application.

**Average client value:** $3,200-$8,400/year in recovered benefits per household.

## 1. Intake Script

**Setting:** In-person, phone, or video call. Allow 30-45 minutes for initial intake. Have the client bring (or have ready): recent pay stubs, tax return, lease/mortgage statement, utility bills, any denial letters.

**Opening:**

> "Thank you for reaching out. I'm a benefits navigator and my job is to make sure you're getting every program and resource you're entitled to. Everything you share with me is confidential and used only to determine your eligibility. There are no wrong answers — the more accurate the picture, the more I can help you. Let's start with some basics."

### Section A: Personal Information

| # | Question | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| A1 | What is your full legal name? | As it appears on government ID |
| A2 | Date of birth? | Age affects SSI, Medicare, etc. |
| A3 | What is your Social Security Number? | Only if needed for specific applications. Can defer to application stage. |
| A4 | What state do you live in? City and county? | Critical — benefits vary dramatically by state and even county |
| A5 | How long have you lived at your current address? | Residency requirements vary (30 days to 12 months) |
| A6 | Are you a U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen? | Affects SNAP, Medicaid, TANF. Qualified aliens (green card 5+ years) eligible for most programs. Refugees/asylees eligible immediately. |
| A7 | Do you have a valid government-issued ID? | If not, this becomes the first task |
| A8 | What is your preferred language? | Many offices have translation services |

### Section B: Household Composition

| # | Question | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| B1 | How many people live in your household? | Include everyone who shares meals/residence |
| B2 | For each person: name, age, relationship to you? | List on a separate sheet |
| B3 | Are any household members under 18? | Unlocks WIC, CHIP, school lunch, CTC |
| B4 | Are any household members pregnant? | Automatic Medicaid in most states, WIC priority |
| B5 | Are any household members over 60? | Senior SNAP benefits, Medicare, senior housing |
| B6 | Are any household members disabled? | Opens SSI/SSDI, Medicaid, housing priority |
| B7 | Is anyone in the household a veteran? | VA benefits, HUD-VASH, veteran preference |
| B8 | Is anyone currently in school (K-12 or college)? | Free/reduced lunch, Pell Grant, work-study |

### Section C: Employment & Income

| # | Question | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| C1 | What is your current employment status? (Employed full-time, part-time, unemployed, self-employed, gig work, seasonal) | Determines UI eligibility |
| C2 | If unemployed: When did you lose your job? | UI filing deadline is typically within 1-4 weeks of separation |
| C3 | If unemployed: Why did you leave? (Laid off, fired, quit, company closed, contract ended, medical) | "Quit" generally disqualifies from UI unless for good cause |
| C4 | What was your most recent gross monthly income? | Before taxes and deductions |
| C5 | What is your current gross monthly household income from ALL sources? | Wages, tips, gig income, child support received, alimony, rental income, investment income |
| C6 | Did you earn at least $2,000 in the past 12-18 months from W-2 employment? | UI minimum earnings threshold (varies by state) |
| C7 | Are you receiving any unemployment benefits now? | If yes, how much per week and when do they expire? |
| C8 | Did you file taxes last year? What was your AGI? | Needed for ACA subsidies, EITC, CTC |
| C9 | Do you have any self-employment or 1099 income? | May qualify for PUA-type programs if available, affects SNAP calculations differently |

### Section D: Assets & Financial Situation

| # | Question | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| D1 | How much do you have in checking/savings combined? | SNAP asset limit: $2,750 ($4,250 if elderly/disabled) in non-expanded states. Many states eliminated asset test. |
| D2 | Do you own your home? Rent? Live with someone? | Affects housing programs and SNAP shelter deduction |
| D3 | What is your monthly rent or mortgage payment? | Used for SNAP shelter deduction calculation |
| D4 | Do you own a vehicle? Year/make/value? | Some states still count vehicle assets for SNAP |
| D5 | Do you have any retirement accounts (401k, IRA)? | Generally excluded from asset tests but varies |
| D6 | Do you have any outstanding debts or bills in collections? | Affects prioritization of which programs to pursue first |
| D7 | Have you received any lump-sum payments recently? (Severance, settlement, inheritance) | Can temporarily affect eligibility |

### Section E: Health & Insurance

| # | Question | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| E1 | Do you currently have health insurance? (Employer, COBRA, ACA marketplace, Medicaid, Medicare, VA, none) | Determines health coverage pathway |
| E2 | If you lost employer coverage: When did it end? | COBRA election period is 60 days. Special enrollment period for ACA is also 60 days. |
| E3 | Does anyone in the household have a chronic condition, disability, or regular medication needs? | Prioritizes Medicaid/Medicare applications |
| E4 | Is anyone pregnant or planning to become pregnant? | Pregnancy Medicaid has higher income limits in most states |
| E5 | Do you have any unpaid medical bills? | Separate issue — may refer to medical billing advocate |

### Section F: Housing & Utilities

| # | Question | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| F1 | What type of housing? (House, apartment, mobile home, shelter, staying with someone, homeless) | Homeless status opens additional programs |
| F2 | Are you behind on rent or facing eviction? | Emergency rental assistance may be available |
| F3 | Are you behind on utilities? Which ones? How much? | LIHEAP, utility company hardship programs |
| F4 | What are your average monthly utility costs? (Electric, gas, water, phone/internet) | LIHEAP eligibility and SNAP utility allowance |
| F5 | Do you feel safe in your current housing? | If DV situation, specialized resources available |

### Section G: Education & Training

| # | Question | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| G1 | What is your highest education level? | Affects job training program eligibility |
| G2 | Are you interested in going back to school or getting trained in a new field? | WIOA, Pell Grants, state training programs |
| G3 | Have you used GI Bill benefits? (If veteran) | May have unused education benefits |
| G4 | Do you have any professional licenses or certifications? | Helps with workforce board referrals |

### Section H: Current Benefits

| # | Question | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|
| H1 | Are you currently receiving ANY government benefits? List all. | Prevents duplicate applications, identifies gaps |
| H2 | Have you applied for and been denied any benefits in the past 12 months? | May be able to appeal or circumstances may have changed |
| H3 | Have you ever received SNAP, TANF, or Medicaid before? | Existing accounts make reapplication faster |

**Closing:**

> "Thank you — this gives me a clear picture. Based on what you've shared, I'm going to run an analysis to identify every program you may qualify for. I'll come back to you within [24-48 hours / our next meeting] with a prioritized list and we'll start applications together. Do you have any questions right now?"

## 3. Program Reference Guide

### Quick Reference: Top 20 Government Benefits Programs

---

### 3.1 Unemployment Insurance (UI)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Weekly cash payments to workers who lost jobs through no fault of their own |
| **Federal/State** | State-administered, federally guided |
| **Eligibility** | Worked and earned minimum wages in "base period" (typically first 4 of last 5 completed calendar quarters). Must be laid off / separated through no fault. Must be able and available to work. Must actively seek work. |
| **Income Limits** | No income limit to file, but earnings reduce benefits. Minimum base period earnings vary by state ($1,000-$5,000+). |
| **Benefit Amount** | Typically 40-60% of previous weekly wage, capped by state maximum. Range: $150-$823/week depending on state. |
| **Duration** | 12-26 weeks (most states 26). Extended benefits during high unemployment. |
| **How to Apply** | State workforce/unemployment website. File within 1 week of separation. |
| **Key Documents** | SSN, driver's license, employer info (name, address, dates, reason for separation), bank info for direct deposit |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Missing the filing deadline. Saying you "quit" when you were constructively discharged. Not documenting job search activities. |
| **Pro Tip** | If denied, ALWAYS appeal. Roughly 50% of appeals succeed. The appeal hearing is your chance to present evidence. |

---

### 3.2 SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Monthly food benefits loaded onto an EBT card |
| **Federal/State** | Federal program, state-administered |
| **Eligibility** | Gross monthly income at or below 130% FPL ($1,644/month for individual, $2,230 for 2, $3,402 for 4 in 2025-2026). Net income at or below 100% FPL. Most states use Broad-Based Categorical Eligibility (BBCE) which raises gross limit to 200% FPL and eliminates asset test. |
| **Asset Limit** | $2,750 ($4,250 if elderly/disabled). Eliminated in 40+ states via BBCE. |
| **Benefit Amount** | Maximum: $292/month (individual), $536 (household of 2), $967 (household of 4). Actual amount depends on income after deductions. Minimum benefit: $23/month. |
| **Duration** | Recertified every 6-12 months. No time limit for most (ABAWDs without dependents: 3 months in 3 years unless working 80+ hrs/month or in training). |
| **How to Apply** | State SNAP office, online portal, or community organizations. Many states allow phone interviews. |
| **Key Documents** | ID, SSN for all household members, proof of income (pay stubs, benefit letters), rent/mortgage receipts, utility bills |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Not reporting all household members. Forgetting to include utility costs (which increase benefits via shelter deduction). Not recertifying on time. |
| **Pro Tip** | Apply for expedited SNAP (within 7 days) if household has less than $150 in monthly gross income AND liquid assets combined, OR if monthly rent/mortgage + utilities exceed monthly income + assets. |

---

### 3.3 Medicaid / ACA Marketplace Subsidies

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Free or low-cost health coverage (Medicaid) or subsidized marketplace insurance (ACA) |
| **Federal/State** | Medicaid: Federal/state. ACA: Federal with state marketplaces. |
| **Eligibility — Medicaid** | In expansion states (40 states + DC): income up to 138% FPL ($20,783/year individual, $28,208/year couple, $42,058/year family of 4). Non-expansion states: Much more restrictive — often only pregnant women, children, disabled, elderly. |
| **Eligibility — ACA Subsidies** | Income 100-400% FPL (enhanced subsidies currently make premiums $0-$50/month for lower incomes). |
| **Benefit Amount** | Medicaid: $0 premiums, $0 or minimal copays. ACA subsidies: Can reduce premiums to $0-$50/month for Silver plans with cost-sharing reductions. |
| **Duration** | Medicaid: Continuous coverage through annual renewal. ACA: Annual enrollment or special enrollment period (60 days from qualifying event like job loss). |
| **How to Apply** | Healthcare.gov (federal marketplace), state marketplace, or state Medicaid office. Can apply anytime for Medicaid. ACA has open enrollment Nov-Jan + special enrollment periods. |
| **Key Documents** | SSN, income proof, immigration status docs if applicable, current coverage info |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Not applying during the 60-day special enrollment period after losing employer coverage. Falling into the "coverage gap" in non-expansion states (too much for Medicaid, too little for ACA subsidies). |
| **Pro Tip** | Job loss triggers a Special Enrollment Period for ACA — you do NOT have to wait for open enrollment. Apply at Healthcare.gov within 60 days of losing coverage. |

---

### 3.4 TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Monthly cash assistance for families with children |
| **Federal/State** | Federal block grant, state-designed programs. Name varies by state (CalWORKs in CA, TAFDC in MA, etc.) |
| **Eligibility** | Must have children under 18 (or pregnant). Very low income (thresholds vary wildly by state — often 30-50% FPL). Must participate in work activities. |
| **Asset Limit** | $1,000-$10,000 depending on state. Some states have no asset test. |
| **Benefit Amount** | $200-$800/month for family of 3 depending on state. (Mississippi: ~$170. California: ~$878.) |
| **Duration** | Federal 60-month lifetime limit. Some states shorter (Arizona: 12 months). Some states exempt certain months. |
| **How to Apply** | State welfare/human services office. Often same application as SNAP and Medicaid. |
| **Key Documents** | Same as SNAP plus proof of children (birth certificates), proof of residence |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Strict work requirements — must participate in work activities within 30-90 days. Sanctions for non-compliance can reduce or eliminate benefits. |
| **Pro Tip** | Many states offer "diversion payments" — a one-time lump sum ($1,000-$3,000) instead of monthly TANF if the client just needs help through a temporary crisis. This does NOT count toward the 60-month lifetime limit in most states. Ask about it. |

---

### 3.5 LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Help paying heating and cooling bills. Some states also cover water bills. |
| **Federal/State** | Federal funding, state-administered |
| **Eligibility** | Generally income at or below 150% FPL or 60% of state median income (whichever is higher). Priority to elderly, disabled, and families with young children. |
| **Benefit Amount** | One-time payment of $200-$1,000+ per heating/cooling season depending on state and need. Some states offer ongoing monthly assistance. Crisis grants available for shutoff emergencies. |
| **Duration** | Must apply each season. Typically October-March for heating, May-September for cooling. |
| **How to Apply** | Local Community Action Agency or state energy assistance office. Search at energyhelp.us or call 211. |
| **Key Documents** | ID, SSN, income proof, utility bills (or shut-off notice for crisis assistance), lease showing address |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Funds run out fast — apply as soon as the season opens. Many people miss the cooling season assistance. |
| **Pro Tip** | If a client has a shutoff notice, apply for CRISIS LIHEAP which is processed in 24-48 hours instead of weeks. Also ask the utility company about their own hardship programs — most have them and they stack with LIHEAP. |

---

### 3.6 Section 8 / Housing Choice Vouchers

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Rental subsidy — tenant pays ~30% of income toward rent, voucher covers the rest up to a payment standard |
| **Federal/State** | Federal (HUD), administered by local Public Housing Authorities (PHAs) |
| **Eligibility** | Income at or below 50% of area median income (AMI). PHAs must allocate 75% of vouchers to households at or below 30% AMI. |
| **Benefit Amount** | Pays the difference between 30% of the tenant's adjusted income and the PHA payment standard. Can be worth $500-$2,000+/month depending on local rents. |
| **Duration** | Ongoing as long as eligible and compliant. Annual income recertification. |
| **How to Apply** | Contact local PHA. Waiting lists are typically years long (2-8 years). Many are closed. |
| **Key Documents** | ID, SSN for all household members, income proof, criminal background check (PHA discretion) |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Waiting lists close unpredictably and may only open for days. When a list opens, apply IMMEDIATELY. |
| **Pro Tip** | Apply to MULTIPLE PHAs — you are not limited to one. Also check for preference categories (veteran, disabled, homeless, DV survivor, working family) that move you up the list. Project-based vouchers at specific properties often have shorter waits. |

---

### 3.7 WIC (Women, Infants, and Children)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Nutritional support for pregnant/postpartum women, infants, and children under 5 |
| **Federal/State** | Federal, state-administered |
| **Eligibility** | Pregnant, breastfeeding (up to 1 year postpartum), non-breastfeeding (up to 6 months postpartum), infants and children under 5. Income at or below 185% FPL. Automatically eligible if on SNAP, Medicaid, or TANF. |
| **Benefit Amount** | Monthly food benefits: ~$35-$75/month for women/children, up to ~$130/month for fully formula-fed infants. Also provides breastfeeding support, nutrition education, and healthcare referrals. |
| **Duration** | Until income exceeds limits or child turns 5, whichever comes first. Must recertify every 6 months. |
| **How to Apply** | Local WIC clinic. Find locations at signupwic.com or call state WIC office. |
| **Key Documents** | ID, proof of address, income proof (or proof of SNAP/Medicaid/TANF enrollment), proof of pregnancy or child's age |
| **Pro Tip** | WIC is one of the most underutilized programs — only about 57% of eligible people participate. It does NOT count as "public charge" for immigration purposes. Fathers and guardians can apply on behalf of children. |

---

### 3.8 SSI (Supplemental Security Income)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Monthly cash payment for disabled, blind, or elderly (65+) individuals with very low income and assets |
| **Federal/State** | Federal (SSA), some states provide supplements |
| **Eligibility** | Disabled (unable to engage in substantial gainful activity for 12+ months), blind, or age 65+. Countable income below federal benefit rate. Assets below $2,000 individual / $3,000 couple. |
| **Benefit Amount** | Federal max: $943/month individual, $1,415/month couple (2025). Many states add $10-$400+ supplement. Reduced by countable income. |
| **Duration** | Ongoing with periodic reviews (every 1-7 years depending on expected improvement). |
| **How to Apply** | SSA office in person or by phone (1-800-772-1213). Online for age-based SSI. |
| **Key Documents** | SSN, birth certificate, proof of citizenship, medical records, income/asset documentation, living arrangement info |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Initial denial rate is ~65%. ALWAYS appeal. Gather thorough medical documentation BEFORE applying. |
| **Pro Tip** | If denied, file appeal within 60 days. The 4-step process: Reconsideration → ALJ Hearing → Appeals Council → Federal Court. Most wins happen at ALJ hearing. Consider hiring a disability attorney — they work on contingency (25% of back pay, capped at $7,200). |

---

### 3.9 SSDI (Social Security Disability Insurance)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Monthly disability payments for workers who paid into Social Security |
| **Federal/State** | Federal (SSA) |
| **Eligibility** | Must have worked and earned enough "work credits" (generally 5 of the last 10 years). Must be unable to perform substantial gainful activity (SGA) for 12+ months. SGA limit: $1,550/month (2025). No asset test. |
| **Benefit Amount** | Based on lifetime earnings. Average: $1,537/month. Range: $800-$3,800/month. |
| **Duration** | Ongoing with periodic medical reviews. Converts to retirement benefits at full retirement age. |
| **How to Apply** | SSA office, phone, or online at ssa.gov. 5-month waiting period from disability onset. |
| **Key Documents** | SSN, complete medical records, work history (last 15 years), medications list, doctors' contact info |
| **Pro Tip** | SSI and SSDI are NOT mutually exclusive — some people qualify for both ("concurrent benefits") if their SSDI amount is low. After 24 months on SSDI, you automatically qualify for Medicare. |

---

### 3.10 WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act) Training Funds

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Pays for job training, certifications, and education for unemployed or underemployed workers |
| **Federal/State** | Federal, administered through local American Job Centers (AJCs) |
| **Eligibility** | Adults (18+): Priority to low-income, public assistance recipients, and veterans. Dislocated workers: Recently laid off, self-employed and closed business, or displaced homemakers. Youth (16-24): Low-income with barriers to employment. |
| **Benefit Amount** | Pays for training programs, certifications, and sometimes supportive services (transportation, childcare). Individual Training Accounts typically $5,000-$10,000. Some states allow more. |
| **Duration** | Training typically 6-24 months depending on program. |
| **How to Apply** | Visit your local American Job Center. Find one at careeronestop.org. |
| **Key Documents** | ID, SSN, income proof, layoff notice or unemployment documentation |
| **Pro Tip** | WIOA can fund CDL training ($3,000-$7,000), IT certifications ($2,000-$5,000), healthcare certifications ($3,000-$8,000), and many other high-demand fields. The training must be on the state's Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL). Ask your AJC counselor what's on the list for your area. |

---

### 3.11 Pell Grants

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Federal grant (not a loan) for undergraduate education |
| **Federal/State** | Federal (Department of Education) |
| **Eligibility** | Financial need based on FAFSA. Must not have a bachelor's degree. Must be enrolled at least half-time at an eligible institution. Expected Family Contribution (EFC) / Student Aid Index (SAI) determines amount. |
| **Benefit Amount** | Up to $7,395/year (2025-2026). Amount based on SAI, cost of attendance, and enrollment status. |
| **Duration** | Up to 12 semesters (6 years full-time equivalent) of Pell eligibility. |
| **How to Apply** | FAFSA at studentaid.gov. Opens October 1 each year. |
| **Key Documents** | SSN, tax return (or non-filing statement), FSA ID |
| **Pro Tip** | The FAFSA Simplification Act (effective 2024-25) means more people qualify. If client's income is at or below 175% FPL or they receive SNAP/TANF/SSI/Medicaid, they likely get maximum Pell. Can be used at community colleges, trade schools, and 4-year universities. |

---

### 3.12 COBRA Subsidies / Continuation Coverage

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Allows you to keep your employer health insurance for up to 18-36 months after leaving a job |
| **Federal/State** | Federal (DOL) — applies to employers with 20+ employees. Many states have "mini-COBRA" for smaller employers. |
| **Eligibility** | Lost group health coverage due to qualifying event (job loss, reduced hours). Voluntary or involuntary termination (except gross misconduct). |
| **Cost** | Full premium + 2% admin fee. Often $500-$2,000+/month. No ongoing federal subsidies currently (ARP subsidies expired). |
| **Duration** | 18 months (job loss/reduced hours). 36 months (death of employee, divorce, dependent aging out). |
| **How to Apply** | Employer must send election notice within 14 days. You have 60 days to elect. Can be retroactive. |
| **Pro Tip** | COBRA is almost always more expensive than ACA marketplace plans with subsidies. Run the numbers on Healthcare.gov FIRST. The only reason to choose COBRA: mid-treatment with a specialist who's in-network on your employer plan but not on any marketplace plan, or you've already met your deductible for the year. |

---

### 3.13 Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **What** | Refundable tax credit for low-to-moderate income workers. You get money back even if you owe no tax. |
| **Federal/State** | Federal + 31 states have state EITC |
| **Eligibility** | Must have earned income. AGI limits (2025): ~$18,591 (single, no kids), ~$56,004 (married, 3+ kids). Must file taxes. Investment income below ~$11,600. |
| **Benefit Amount** | Up to $632 (no kids), $4,213 (1 child), $6,960 (2 children), $7,830 (3+ children) for tax year 2025. |
| **Duration** | Annual — claim on tax return each year you qualify |
| **How to Apply** | File federal tax return with Schedule EIC. Free filing through IRS Free File, VITA sites, or GetYourRefund.org. |
| **Pro Tip** | EITC is the single largest cash benefit many working families receive, but ~20% of eligible filers don't claim it. If client has any earned income and low-moderate AGI, run the numbers. Also check for state EITC which adds 3-45% on top of federal EITC depending on state. |

## 6. Escalation Guide

### When to Refer to Legal Aid

Refer the client to a legal aid attorney (free for low-income clients) when:

- **Denied SSI/SSDI and filing an appeal** — Disability attorneys work on contingency and dramatically improve outcomes at ALJ hearings
- **Facing eviction** — Legal representation reduces eviction by 80%+ in many studies
- **Benefits terminated without proper notice** — Due process violations require legal intervention
- **Immigration-related benefits questions** — Wrong advice can trigger "public charge" consequences
- **Debt collectors threatening to garnish benefits** — Most government benefits are protected from garnishment by federal law
- **TANF/SNAP sanctions that seem improper** — Right to fair hearing with legal representation
- **Employer misclassified them as independent contractor** — Affects UI eligibility and requires legal filing
- **Workers' compensation claim** — Interacts with SSDI and requires specialized legal knowledge

**How to find legal aid:**
- LawHelp.org — directory of free legal aid by state
- Legal Services Corporation (LSC) — lsc.gov/find-legal-aid
- State bar association lawyer referral service
- Local law school clinics
- 211 hotline

### When to Refer to a Licensed Social Worker

Refer when:

- **Mental health crisis** — Client expresses hopelessness, suicidal ideation, or severe anxiety
- **Domestic violence** — National DV Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
- **Substance abuse** — SAMHSA Helpline: 1-800-662-4357
- **Child welfare concerns** — Mandatory reporting may apply depending on your state
- **Hoarding or self-neglect** — Especially in elderly clients
- **Complex family dynamics affecting benefits** — Custody, guardianship, conservatorship issues
- **Housing instability with children** — McKinney-Vento liaison for school-age children

### When to Refer to Government Office Directly

Refer when:

- **Application has been pending beyond normal processing time** (SNAP: 30 days, Medicaid: 45 days, UI: 2-3 weeks)
- **Client received an overpayment notice** — Must respond directly to the agency
- **Fraud investigation** — Do NOT coach the client; refer them to legal aid AND the agency
- **Complex eligibility determination** — Unusual circumstances that AI and standard guidelines cannot resolve
- **Inter-state benefit transfers** — Moving between states during active benefits
- **Identity theft affecting benefits** — Agency must lock the account and issue new credentials

## 7. Pricing Guide

### Model A: Free / Government-Funded (Community Organizations)

**Best for:** Organizations receiving CSBG, WIOA, or other government/foundation funding.

- Offer benefits navigation as a free community service
- Revenue comes from grants, not clients
- Track outcomes (total benefits enrolled, dollar value recovered) for grant reporting
- **Your value prop to funders:** "For every $1 of navigator salary, we recover $15-$25 in benefits for community members"

### Model B: Flat Fee Per Session ($50-$75)

**Best for:** Independent navigators serving working-poor and lower-middle-class clients.

| Service | Fee |
|---------|-----|
| Initial intake + eligibility assessment (45-60 min) | $50 |
| Single program application assistance (30-60 min) | $50 |
| Appeal or dispute assistance (60-90 min) | $75 |
| Recertification help (15-30 min) | $25 |

**Payment terms:** Due at time of service. Cash, Venmo, CashApp, Zelle accepted.

**Sliding scale:** Offer 50% discount for clients with zero income. You will make it up in volume and referrals.

### Model C: Comprehensive Package ($100-$200)

**Best for:** Navigators with experience who can deliver high-value multi-program enrollment.

| Package | Fee | Includes |
|---------|-----|----------|
| **Single Program** | $100 | Intake + eligibility check + one application + follow-up until decision |
| **Full Benefits Audit** | $200 | Intake + eligibility for ALL programs + up to 5 applications + 90 days follow-up |
| **Complex Case** | $200 | SSI/SSDI prep, appeal assistance, or multi-barrier cases requiring 3+ hours |
| **Monthly Retainer** | $50/mo | Ongoing monitoring, recertification reminders, and one ad-hoc request per month |

### Model D: Value-Based / Success Fee

**Best for:** Experienced navigators confident in their success rate.

- Charge 3-5% of the first year's estimated benefit value
- Example: Client enrolled in SNAP ($400/mo) + Medicaid (value $500/mo) + LIHEAP ($800/year) = ~$11,600/year in benefits. Your fee: $348-$580.
- Offer payment plan: $50 upfront + remainder over 3 months
- **Ethical note:** Always inform the client what they could get for free (they can apply themselves). Your value is speed, accuracy, and maximizing the total package.

### Pricing Best Practices

1. **Never charge for information** — telling someone what they qualify for should be free or very low cost
2. **Charge for your time and expertise** in completing applications, navigating bureaucracy, and following up
3. **Never guarantee outcomes** — you can guarantee your effort and expertise, not the agency's decision
4. **Get it in writing** — a simple 1-page service agreement protects both parties
5. **Consider pro bono hours** — 10-20% of your caseload as free cases builds goodwill and referral pipeline
6. **Track your stats** — "I've helped 47 clients recover an average of $6,200/year in benefits" is your best marketing

## Appendix D: Common Denial Reasons and How to Prevent Them

| Program | Common Denial Reason | Prevention |
|---------|---------------------|------------|
| UI | "Quit voluntarily" | Document constructive discharge, hostile conditions, or good cause |
| UI | "Insufficient base period earnings" | Check alternate base period eligibility |
| SNAP | "Over income" | Ensure ALL deductions are claimed (shelter, dependent care, medical) |
| SNAP | "Failed to complete interview" | Calendar the interview date; call if notice doesn't arrive |
| Medicaid | "Over income" | Verify MAGI calculation; check for pregnancy or disability exceptions |
| Medicaid | "Failed to verify citizenship" | Submit passport, birth certificate, or naturalization docs proactively |
| TANF | "Non-cooperation with work requirements" | Document good cause for any missed activities |
| SSI/SSDI | "Not disabled under SSA definition" | Get detailed functional capacity statements from treating doctors |
| Section 8 | "Waitlist closed" | Apply to multiple PHAs; check quarterly for open lists |
| LIHEAP | "Funds exhausted" | Apply on opening day of the season |
| WIOA | "Training not on ETPL" | Verify training provider is on Eligible Training Provider List before enrolling |
| Pell Grant | "Did not complete FAFSA" | Help client complete FAFSA; use IRS Data Retrieval Tool |
